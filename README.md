# Mystic Treatises

### A Modern Devotional Adaptation of Isaac of Nineveh

> *"Humility is the garment of divinity."* — Isaac of Nineveh

## About This Project

**The Way of Mercy** is a collection of one hundred daily devotional readings adapted from **Isaac of Nineveh's *Mystic Treatises***.

This is **not** a new translation.

It is **not** a commentary.

It is **not** an academic study.

Instead, it is an attempt to allow one of Christianity's greatest contemplative voices to speak naturally to modern readers.

Each devotional preserves the theological movement, contemplative atmosphere, and pastoral heart of Isaac's original writings while rendering them in clear, contemporary English.

The goal is simple:

> **Remain faithful to Isaac's thought while removing the distance created by centuries of language.**

Rather than explaining Isaac, these readings allow him to speak directly to the reader—as though a wise spiritual elder were sitting beside us, gently inviting us toward prayer, humility, mercy, and the love of God.

---

## Editorial Philosophy

This adaptation follows a few guiding principles:

* Preserve Isaac's theology as faithfully as possible.
* Modernize the language completely.
* Remove unnecessary repetition while preserving contemplative rhythm.
* Maintain Isaac's voice—gentle, spacious, humble, and hopeful.
* Never dilute his emphasis on mercy, humility, repentance, stillness, and divine love.
* End every devotional in hope.

Whenever possible, Isaac's recurring imagery has been retained:

* Light
* Fire
* Silence
* The Heart
* Tears
* Prayer
* The Desert
* Living Water
* Mercy
* Stillness
* Divine Love

---

## Structure

Each devotional follows a consistent format:

```text
Title

A short aphorism

Four to five contemplative paragraphs

Breath Prayer
```

The devotional format intentionally slows the reader down.

This is a book meant to be prayed rather than hurried.

---

## Why This Project Exists

Many readers discover Isaac through translations that are historically important but linguistically distant.

While those translations remain invaluable, they often require patient interpretation before Isaac's remarkable pastoral warmth begins to shine through.

This project seeks to bridge that gap.

The hope is that readers will encounter not merely an ancient monk, but a living spiritual companion whose words continue to illuminate the Christian journey fourteen centuries later.

---

## Intended Audience

This book may especially resonate with readers who appreciate the Christian contemplative tradition, including:

* Julian of Norwich
* The Cloud of Unknowing
* Brother Lawrence
* Teresa of Ávila
* John of the Cross
* Thomas Merton
* Henri Nouwen

It is also written for anyone longing for a quieter, deeper life with God.

---

## Source Material

This work is adapted from ***Mystic Treatises*** by **Isaac of Nineveh (Isaac the Syrian)**.

Every effort has been made to preserve the spirit and theological intent of the original while presenting it in accessible modern English.

Readers interested in Isaac's complete writings are encouraged to read the original translations alongside this devotional adaptation.

---

## A Final Word

Isaac believed that the goal of the spiritual life was never the accumulation of knowledge, nor even the perfection of religious discipline.

Its end is communion.

To become so captivated by the love of God that the self quietly yields its place, and the Beloved becomes the center of everything.

If these pages help awaken that desire, then they have fulfilled their purpose.

*"Remain near the Beloved."*
