## 100 — The Freedom of the Humble

> *True humility is not achieved by striving to appear small, but by becoming so filled with Christ that the self no longer seeks its own importance.*

Do not be discouraged when you discover that true humility is beyond your own power to produce. It is not something that grows from self-contempt, nor is it the reward for a few moments of sorrow or tears. Humility is a gift of the Holy Spirit. It is the quiet strength God plants within a heart that has learned to surrender itself completely to Him. As fire transformed the apostles into fearless witnesses, so this hidden grace transforms the soul until humility becomes as natural as breathing.

Do not imagine that a single victory, a beautiful prayer, or an act of self-denial means you have reached the summit. The journey is longer, and the gift is deeper. Humility is not one virtue among many. It is the fragrance that arises when every virtue has been gathered into love. When the Spirit dwells freely within the heart, the soul no longer struggles to appear humble. It simply forgets itself. It has become so occupied with the beauty of God that there is no room left for pride.

Perhaps you ask, "How can I receive such a gift?" Then lift your eyes to Christ. Do not begin by examining yourself. Begin by contemplating Him. See how the Lord of all chose poverty instead of privilege, service instead of honor, and the cross instead of self-preservation. He could say that the ruler of this world had no claim upon Him because nothing within Him sought its own glory. Walk in His footsteps. Let go, little by little, of every desire to be admired, defended, or exalted. As you become like Him, His own life will quietly take root within you.

Do not lose heart because you are not yet what you long to become. The One who calls you to humility is the One who gives it. Your task is not to manufacture holiness but to remain close to Christ with an open and willing heart. Stay near Him in prayer. Follow Him in love. Entrust yourself to His Spirit day after day. In time, without your noticing, you will find that the need to lift yourself up has faded, and a deeper joy has taken its place. Then you will rest, not in what you have become, but in the One whose gentle life has become your own.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** *Make me like Christ.*

> **Exhale:** *Teach me His humility.*
