## 91 — The Freedom of an Undivided Heart

> *The heart that belongs wholly to God need not be ruled by fear.*

As long as your heart remains turned toward God, you will remember that this life is passing away and that another life is already drawing near. Do not let this thought make you afraid. Let it make you free. When your hope rests beyond this world, its honors lose their hold, its comforts no longer rule you, and even suffering cannot rob you of your peace. Entrust your life into God's hands, and you will discover a courage that does not come from your own strength. Whatever trials meet you along the way, receive them with quiet confidence, believing that the hidden wisdom of divine love is at work where your eyes cannot yet see.

But be watchful over your heart. It does not drift toward fear all at once. A small attachment, a longing for greater comfort, an anxious desire to preserve yourself—these quietly awaken self-love. Soon your thoughts become filled with protecting what cannot last, and the freedom you once knew gives way to endless calculation. Fear grows wherever your treasure settles. If your heart clings to the passing world, it must also carry the world's burdens. But if your treasure is God, your soul will remain light, even when the road is hard.

Understand that the heart cannot breathe the air of heaven while it is buried beneath the weight of earthly cares. As clear water washes away what is unclean, so troubles often cleanse the soul from loves that have quietly taken root. Do not despise the trials that unsettle you. They may be loosening the grip of things that have kept your heart from deeper peace. What feels like loss may become a hidden mercy, drawing you back into the spaciousness of God's presence.

When temptation, sorrow, or hardship overtakes you, do not waste your strength accusing others, resisting the lesson, or questioning the goodness of God. Instead, run to Him as a frightened child runs into the embrace of a loving parent. Let every trouble become another prayer. Humility opens the door that pride keeps shut. When you acknowledge your need, grace enters quietly and begins its healing work. Even the gift of seeing your own weakness is itself a mercy from God. Receive that mercy with gratitude, and rest in the certainty that the One who reveals your heart does so only because He desires to fill it with His own unfailing love.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** *My heart belongs to You.*

> **Exhale:** *In Your love I rest.*
