## 89 — Become Like a Little Child

> *The heart that trusts God simply discovers a wisdom that cleverness alone can never reach.*

If you desire the life of God, do not begin by trusting your own understanding. Come before him with the heart of a little child. Lay aside the need to master every mystery, to solve every question, or to secure yourself by your own wisdom. There is a knowledge that grows through careful thought, and it has its proper place. But there is another knowledge that is born only through trust. This wisdom cannot be learned by cleverness. It is received by those who surrender themselves to God.

Ask for this gift without growing weary. Pray until faith becomes more than an idea. When your heart truly begins to rest in God's care, you will discover a quiet confidence that no argument can produce. Then your anxieties begin to loosen their grip, not because every danger has disappeared, but because you know whose hands hold your life. Faith does not remove every storm. It teaches the soul to remain steady within it.

Do not burden yourself by trying to force spiritual understanding through endless reasoning. The deepest things of God are simple. They are revealed to the heart that has become quiet, pure, and undivided. As a child receives the warmth of the sun without knowing how light is made, so the humble soul receives the light of God without needing to possess it. Purity does not manufacture this light. It simply makes room for it.

Entrust yourself wholly to the Lord. Once you have placed your life into his hands, resist the temptation to take it back through worry. Say within yourself, "The One who cares for me knows what I do not." Then you will begin to notice how often his unseen mercy has quietly surrounded you. You will recognize doors he closed to protect you, dangers you never saw, and gentle provisions that arrived before you even knew your need. What once seemed like chance will become a testimony to his faithful care.

Grace teaches patiently. It first strengthens your trust through small mercies, then prepares you for greater trials, never abandoning you as it leads you onward. Even your temptations become lessons, revealing both your weakness and God's strength. Do not fear this gentle schooling. The Father does not train you to prove your worth, but to deepen your confidence in his love. Become like a little child, and you will discover that you have never walked alone.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** *Teach me childlike trust.*

> **Exhale:** *I rest in Your care.*
