## 86 — A Heart on Fire with Mercy

> *Purity is a heart so filled with mercy that it can no longer turn away from the suffering of any creature.*

The whole spiritual journey may be gathered into three movements: repentance, purity, and perfection. Repentance is the heart turning away from what has wounded love. Purity is compassion flowing freely toward all creation. Perfection is the depth of humility in which you no longer cling to anything as your own, but place yourself and all things into the hands of God.

Do not imagine that a pure heart is merely one untouched by sinful thoughts. A pure heart burns with mercy. It is moved by the suffering of every person, every creature, and even those who oppose what is good. When such a heart sees pain, tears rise without effort. It cannot bear cruelty or indifference. It prays for those who suffer, for those who cause suffering, and for all living things that have no voice with which to plead for themselves.

This mercy is the likeness of God growing within you. God does not compel the world into love. He draws it by love. He gave himself to us, not because he lacked another way to save, but because he desired to reveal the immeasurable tenderness of his heart. Christ accepted suffering freely so that we might know that divine love does not stand far from our pain. It enters our wounds and carries them within itself.

When the saints became filled with this love, the good of others became their own joy. They no longer asked, “What will I keep?” but, “What may I give?” They were willing to surrender comfort, possessions, reputation, and even life itself so that another person might be strengthened. Such love is not born from duty alone. It rises from a heart that has tasted God and discovered that nothing is more precious than becoming a shelter for another.

Ask, then, not merely to become disciplined or knowledgeable, but to become merciful. Let compassion be the measure of your prayer. Let humility empty your hands, and let love fill them again. When your heart begins to embrace the whole creation without distinction, do not be afraid of its tenderness. You are not becoming weak. You are being clothed with God, whose mercy holds every creature and whose love will never cease seeking their healing.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** *Clothe me in Your mercy.*

> **Exhale:** *Let my heart embrace all.*
