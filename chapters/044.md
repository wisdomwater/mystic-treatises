## 44 — The Tears That Become Joy

> *The road of holy sorrow ends in a joy the world cannot give.*

Do not be afraid of the sorrow that draws you toward God. There is a mourning that comes from wounded pride, and there is a mourning that comes from love. The first leaves the heart closed in upon itself. The second opens it to grace. When you grieve because you long for God more than for your own comfort, your tears become a hidden prayer that no words can express.

Do not think these tears are an end in themselves. They are a doorway. Through them the heart is washed clean, and as it is purified, another country begins to appear within. The soul discovers a quiet consolation that does not depend upon changing circumstances. This is the comfort promised by the Lord—not the removal of every hardship, but the gift of His own presence. Those who have tasted this know that even their mourning has become full of hope.

If your tears have not yet come, do not force them. Instead, remain faithful in prayer. Read the Scriptures with a listening heart. Sit in silence before God. Return to Him again and again. Every moment spent in His presence softens the heart, and when the time is right, He Himself will give whatever gift is needed. Grace cannot be manufactured; it can only be received.

The Lord desires not merely to comfort your emotions, but to draw you into His love. There is a place beyond restless striving where the soul rests in Him alone. Walk patiently toward that place. Whether your path is marked by tears or by quiet stillness, trust that His mercy is leading you. Every step taken in love brings you nearer to the joy that will never be taken away.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** *Wash my heart.*

> **Exhale:** *Lead me into Your joy.*
