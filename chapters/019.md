## Day 19 — Blessed Is the One Who Knows His Weakness

> *The beginning of wisdom is not confidence in yourself, but confidence in God's mercy.*

Blessed are you if you have begun to see your own weakness. Do not grieve over this discovery, for it is the beginning of true knowledge. As long as you imagine yourself sufficient, you will pray little and trust yourself much. But when you see how easily fear overtakes you, how quickly your thoughts wander, and how often your strength fails, your heart will turn naturally toward God. Then prayer will no longer be a burden but a necessity, and in that necessity you will discover the fountain of life.

Remain there. Cry out to God without ceasing. He sometimes answers quickly, and at other times He seems to delay. Do not imagine that His delay is indifference. A loving father does not always remove every struggle from his child. At times He allows temptation to remain, not because He delights in your distress, but because He desires that you remain close to the source of your strength. In your need you return to Him, and in returning you are healed more deeply than if the trial had never come.

Do not be surprised, then, when God permits seasons that humble you. Humility is not learned through comfort but through dependence. The Lord allows weakness, misunderstanding, fear, poverty, illness, and many hidden struggles to loosen your trust in yourself. Every trial that bows your heart before Him becomes a teacher of wisdom. The soul that has been humbled becomes gentle. The heart that has been broken becomes spacious enough to receive mercy.

Therefore, when hardship comes, do not accuse God. Quiet your soul before Him. Search your own heart before you judge His ways. Remember always that He is the fountain of justice and compassion. Whatever He permits, He permits for your healing. Whatever He withholds, He withholds in wisdom. Whatever He gives, He gives in love.

Do not seek weakness, but neither flee from the humility it brings. Simply remember that you are a creature in need of your Creator every moment of your life. This remembrance will keep you awake, your heart soft, and your hands lifted in prayer. And the God who gives grace to the humble will draw near to you, filling your weakness with His own strength and your poverty with His inexhaustible mercy.
