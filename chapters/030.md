## 30 — When Prayer Gives Way to Wonder

> *The highest gift of prayer is not more words, but a heart captured by God.*

Do not seek extraordinary experiences, and never imagine that you can command them. No one can decide when to enter the deepest things of God. Those who boast of such power have not yet learned humility. The greatest gifts are never taken by force. They are received only as grace. Your task is not to climb into heaven, but to stand faithfully before God with an open heart.

This is why prayer is such a holy place. When you pray, your mind is gathered from its many distractions and turned toward the One who loves you. Your heart waits at the King's door with hope, asking, seeking, and trusting. There is no hour more fitting for mercy than this. Again and again throughout the Scriptures, God met His servants while they were praying. Not because prayer compels Him to come, but because prayer has already turned the soul toward His presence.

Sometimes, while you are praying, God grants something you did not know to ask for. A quiet light rises within. The burden you carried slips from your hands. Even the prayer itself is forgotten as your heart becomes absorbed in the beauty of God. You are no longer striving to reach Him because, for a brief and gracious moment, you discover that He has already reached you. Words fall silent, not because prayer has ended in failure, but because it has flowered into wonder.

Do not measure your spiritual life by whether such moments come. Many faithful souls spend their lives offering simple prayers that are precious to God. If He grants deeper consolations, receive them with gratitude and humility. If He does not, continue praying with the same love. The gift is never the goal. God Himself is the goal.

So remain faithful in the ordinary work of prayer. Return each day with your praise, your repentance, your longing, and your trust. Whether He answers with words, with silence, or with a peace beyond understanding, you are already where you need to be: standing before the One whose mercy is always greater than your asking. In time, every true prayer will find its rest in Him.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** *Gather my heart, O Lord.*

> **Exhale:** *Into Your quiet presence.*
