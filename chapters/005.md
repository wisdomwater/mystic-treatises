## 5 — The Soul’s True Health

> *The soul was made for light; what darkens it is not its deepest nature.*

Do not think that the passions are the true shape of your soul. Anger, envy, restless desire, hunger for praise, fear, pride, and troubled thoughts may move within you, but they are not the image in which God made you. They are illnesses that have entered where health was meant to dwell. The soul’s nature is purity, clarity, and light.

When the soul returns to its proper order, it rises toward God. When it abandons that order and lives only through the body, it becomes heavy and wounded. The body has its own needs, and these are not evil. God made each nature with what helps it grow. But when the soul forgets its own hunger and feeds only on what belongs to the body, it becomes restless, like a bird trying to live beneath the earth.

Do not be surprised, then, when contrary thoughts still trouble you. While you are clothed in flesh, struggle will remain. Even those who have walked far in prayer are not beyond watchfulness. Purity of mind does not mean never knowing temptation. It means the heart has become so captivated by divine things that temptation no longer rules as master.

There is a difference between a mind briefly cleansed and a heart deeply healed. The mind may grow quiet through solitude, fasting, and prayer, yet it can quickly be disturbed again. The heart is purified more slowly. It is healed through patience, tears, humility, and long faithfulness. But when the heart has become whole, it does not fear every small disturbance, for it has learned to rest in God.

So be patient with the work of healing. Do not confuse your wounds with your nature. Do not call darkness your home. Beneath every affliction, the soul still remembers the light for which it was made. Return again to God, and the One who created you in beauty will restore you in mercy.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** *You made me for light.*

> **Exhale:** *Restore my heart in mercy.*
