## 20 — Rise Again

> *Do not measure your life by how often you fall, but by how often you return to God.*

Do not despair because you have stumbled. There is a great difference between the soul that grieves over its sin and rises again, and the soul that willingly makes peace with evil. If your heart still longs for God, if you still hate the darkness that has overtaken you, if you still begin again after every failure, then do not surrender to hopelessness. Let this be your confession: "Though I fall, I will rise again. Though I sit in darkness, the Lord Himself will be my light."

The enemy desires nothing more than to persuade you that the struggle is over. He whispers that repeated failure proves you are beyond mercy. Do not believe him. Better to bear the wounds of a long battle than to lay down your arms and give yourself willingly to defeat. Even if your ship seems to sink again and again, build another. Set your sail once more toward God. The Lord sees every effort born of love, and He has compassion on those who refuse to abandon hope.

Yet do not mistake God's mercy for permission to become careless. The same love that lifts you after you fall also calls you away from the path that wounds you. God warns you because He desires your life, not your destruction. He disciplines because He loves. Consider those who were entrusted with much and yet suffered because they treated sin lightly. Let their stories awaken holy reverence within you, not fear that drives you from God, but love that draws you nearer to Him.

Walk, then, with both confidence and humility. Trust completely in God's mercy, yet never become indifferent to the things that separate your heart from Him. Whenever you fail, repent quickly. Whenever you grow weary, begin again. Whenever you are tempted to despair, remember that Christ is more eager to forgive than you are to ask.

Your hope does not rest upon a flawless record but upon the steadfast love of God. As long as there is breath within you, there is still a path home. Keep your face turned toward the light. The One who called you has not ceased calling, and He will not turn away the heart that continues to return to Him.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** *When I fall...*

> **Exhale:** *...raise me again, Lord.*
