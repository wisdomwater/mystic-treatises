## 80 — The Hidden Pearl of Solitude

> *The purpose of solitude is not to escape the world, but to awaken to God.*

Do not enter silence merely because it is an ancient practice or because others speak well of it. If you seek solitude, know why you have come. A traveler endures the hardships of the sea because he keeps his eyes on the harbor. So let your heart remain fixed upon the treasure hidden within this way of life. If you forget the purpose, every sacrifice will seem heavy, your prayers will become burdensome, and your solitude will feel like a prison. But if your hope remains before you, even difficult days will become light.

The gift of solitude is not found first in what you do, but in what slowly happens within you. As the noise of old memories fades, the heart grows quiet. Prayer becomes sweeter than before. Words begin to give way to silence, and silence gives way to a joy that arrives without your calling for it. Like gentle waves rising from a hidden sea, peace begins to flow from depths you did not know existed. The soul is carried beyond the clamor of the world into the stillness of God.

Do not imagine that this comes quickly. Remain faithful in the ordinary disciplines: prayer, watchfulness, fasting, and holy silence. These are not ends in themselves but the soil in which the hidden life grows. The one who perseveres discovers that solitude is not emptiness but communion. God opens the eyes of the heart until the beauty of creation itself becomes a hymn. Night and day lose their anxious divisions as everything begins to speak of the One who made it.

There is a silence that merely closes the mouth, and there is a silence that opens the soul. Seek the second. The first is your effort; the second is God's gift. When the heart has become still enough to receive Him, He gently draws it beyond restless thoughts into a quiet joy that words cannot contain. There the old desires lose their hold, and the love of God becomes the soul's deepest delight.

If this path sometimes feels lonely, do not lose courage. The One you seek is already nearer than your own breath. Persevere with hope. Every hidden prayer, every faithful vigil, every quiet hour offered to God is leading you toward the pearl for which you first set sail. And when you find Him, you will discover that He has been seeking you all along.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** *Draw me into Your silence.*

> **Exhale:** *Lead me to Your hidden joy.*
