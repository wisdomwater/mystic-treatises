## 99 — The Garment of Christ

> *Humility is the clothing that allows the life of Christ to become visible within us.*

When God chose to come near to us, He did not come wrapped in splendor that would overwhelm us. He clothed Himself in humility. The One whom heaven could not contain drew near with gentleness, speaking to us in a human voice so that we would not flee in fear. Learn, then, what humility truly is. It is not weakness. It is not the denial of truth. It is the very garment the Son of God chose to wear. Whoever puts on this same garment begins to resemble Christ, not only in outward behavior, but in the hidden disposition of the heart.

Do not seek honor for yourself. Seek instead to become so hidden in God that His beauty is seen where once your own importance stood. There is a quiet authority that belongs only to the humble. They do not demand respect, yet hearts are drawn toward them. They do not strive to be heard, yet their few words carry peace. Love surrounds them because they have ceased trying to place themselves above anyone else. The fragrance of Christ rests upon them, and even those who cannot explain it sense that they have come near something holy.

Yet do not mistake humility for merely thinking poorly of yourself. It is possible to remember your failures, to rebuke yourself, and still remain occupied with yourself. That is not yet the fullness of humility. True humility is deeper than self-criticism. It no longer needs to prove anything, defend anything, or compare itself with anyone. It has become as natural as breathing. Whether praised or overlooked, honored or forgotten, it remains at peace because it has learned to rest entirely in God.

When this grace begins to live within you, you will no longer measure yourself by your gifts, your accomplishments, or even your progress in the spiritual life. The more God reveals to you, the more you will marvel at His greatness and the less you will think of your own. You may receive wisdom, yet quietly believe you have only begun to learn. You may become rich in grace, yet still see yourself as the least among your brothers and sisters. This is not pretense but freedom. It is the freedom of a heart that has forgotten itself because it has become wholly occupied with the beauty of Christ. There, in that blessed forgetting, divine love finds a home.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** *Clothe me with Christ.*

> **Exhale:** *Teach me true humility.*
