## 7 — The Trial That Becomes Friendship

> *Do not seek temptation, but when love requires endurance, do not turn away.*

Pray that you do not enter the temptations that wound the soul: doubt that darkens faith, pride that trusts its own height, impurity that burns without the guardian of chastity, anger that abuses what is near, and secret thoughts that lead the heart into confusion. Do not ask to be tested by these. Ask God to keep you from the fire that consumes rather than purifies.

But do not flee the troubles that come for the sake of love. There are trials the body bears while the soul is being strengthened. Without them, the care of God is not clearly seen, spiritual wisdom is not learned, and divine love is not deeply planted. Before such trials, a person may pray to God as a stranger. After enduring them without turning away, the soul begins to speak with Him as a friend of the household.

Still, do not enter hardship out of pride, self-exaltation, or foolishness. Pray that if you must suffer, it may be for love of God and not because of reckless thoughts. Pray that His power may become visible in your weakness. Pray that endurance may glorify Him and not feed your own hidden vanity.

When the commandment of God stands before you, do not let fear of suffering make you abandon it. The One who was with Joseph in Egypt, Daniel among the lions, the three youths in the furnace, Peter in prison, and Paul among his accusers will also be with you. He has never left His servants alone in the storm.

So strengthen your heart with the memory of the just. Let their lives be medicine for weak eyes and water for young plants. Above all, remember Christ, who humbled Himself for the life of all. He entered our suffering, carried our sin, and made mercy visible in flesh. Therefore do not be afraid. The trial that comes through love can become the place where God calls you friend.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** *Be with me in the storm.*

> **Exhale:** *Make me Your friend.*
