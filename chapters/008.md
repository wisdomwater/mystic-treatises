## 8 — The Crown You Do Not See

> *Mercy rises higher than justice and makes the heart familiar with God.*

Do not despise the labors that come for the sake of truth. The tears of sowing are followed by the joy of harvest. Bread earned with sweat is sweet to the worker, and endurance for righteousness becomes sweet to the heart that has begun to know Christ.

When a hard word is spoken against you, and you bear it with discernment, you receive a hidden crown. You may not see it. You may not feel honored. Yet if you endure without bitterness, for the sake of love, Christ places upon you the likeness of His own crown of thorns. Blessed is the soul that is crowned and does not know it.

Do not say you have left the world while still quarreling over its smallest comforts. If you have given up the whole world, why fight over a fragment of it? Whatever you cling to for the sake of your own will has become your possession, whether it is gold, praise, control, comfort, or the need to be right.

If you desire mercy, do not practice justice in a narrow way. Give what is yours to the poor, but go further still. Forgive those who wrong you. Let peace spring up in your soul from both sides. Mercy is not only generosity with possessions; it is the heart burning with compassion when another suffers, and refusing even to wound the one who has wounded you.

So keep watch over the small things. Love simplicity. Guard your senses. Seek peace within yourself before seeking to correct the whole world. Many have tried to heal others while neglecting their own wounds, and in the end they fell where they thought they were strong. First let God heal your heart. Then whatever mercy flows from you will carry His life, and not your own restlessness.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** *Crown me with mercy.*

> **Exhale:** *Heal my hidden heart.*
