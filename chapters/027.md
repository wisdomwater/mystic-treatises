## 27 — The Hidden Life

> *The deepest work of God is often hidden from the eyes of the world.*

The desire to be useful is good. Yet there is a usefulness that can quietly steal the soul away from its true calling. It is possible to become so occupied with doing things for God that we neglect simply being with God. Activity, even holy activity, can fill every corner of the heart until silence no longer has room to speak.

There are seasons when love calls us outward, but there are also seasons when love calls us inward. The soul needs a place where no demands can reach it, where no reputation needs defending, where no success must be achieved. In that hidden place we discover that our worth does not depend upon what we accomplish, but upon the One in whose presence we rest.

Isaac speaks boldly about the solitary life because he knew its purpose. Solitude is not withdrawal from love. It is the slow schooling of the heart until God becomes its deepest delight. The quieter the soul becomes, the more clearly it hears the gentle movements of grace. The less it clings to the world, the more freely it can receive the life of heaven.

Most of us are not called to the desert in the literal sense. We have families, work, responsibilities, and neighbors to love. Yet every disciple needs an inner desert—a place where no voice is louder than God's. Whether it is ten minutes in the morning, a quiet walk, or a few moments of stillness before sleep, this hidden sanctuary becomes the wellspring from which every act of love flows.

Do not fear silence. The world teaches us that productivity proves our value. Christ teaches us that abiding bears fruit. The soul that learns to remain with God will eventually carry His peace wherever it goes.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** *Draw me into Your stillness.*

> **Exhale:** *Teach me to abide in You.*
