## 29 — When Prayer Becomes Silence

> *Prayer opens the door, but love leads you into the house.*

There are times when a single word of prayer becomes so full of sweetness that you can do nothing but remain with it. You repeat it, not from effort, but because your heart has found rest there. Then, sometimes, even that word falls away. Not because prayer has failed, but because it has accomplished its purpose. The lips grow still. The heart becomes quiet. The mind ceases its striving. You stand before God without knowing how to speak.

Do not fear this silence or try to escape it by filling it with more words. Prayer is the key that opens the door, but once you have entered the treasury, you no longer linger at the threshold. When the Master of the house comes, every voice becomes still. Even your deepest desires, your tears, your petitions, and your praises give way to a simple awareness of His presence. This is not emptiness. It is fullness too deep for speech.

Yet do not seek this experience for its own sake. It cannot be summoned by determination or produced by spiritual effort. Pure prayer is itself a rare gift, born from a heart that has learned to gather its scattered thoughts and offer itself wholly to God. If distractions come, return gently. If your mind wanders, return again. Faithfulness in ordinary prayer prepares the soul for whatever God desires to give, whether many words or profound silence.

The holy mysteries of God cannot be captured by names or definitions. We borrow words such as prayer, contemplation, knowledge, or revelation, yet these are only signposts pointing toward a reality greater than language. Just as no description can replace the warmth of the sun, no word can contain the life of God. Do not become anxious over what these experiences should be called. Seek only the One to whom they lead.

When the Lord chooses to draw the soul beyond its own efforts, He carries it where it could never go by itself. There the heart rests without striving, not because it has reached the end of God, but because it has been gently gathered into His love. Whether He gives you words or silence, longing or stillness, know that His desire is always the same: to bring you nearer to Himself. Rest there without fear, for His presence is the fulfillment toward which every true prayer has always been leading.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** *Lead me beyond words.*

> **Exhale:** *Into Your quiet love.*
