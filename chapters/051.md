## 51 — Trouble as the Road of Love

> *The soul that draws near to God should not be surprised when the road becomes narrow.*

Do not fear hardship when it comes for the sake of the good. No one truly lets go of lesser things until the heart has begun to believe in something greater than comfort. The soul can endure much when it has tasted, even faintly, the joy that waits beyond passing ease.

Solitude, restraint, and patient endurance are not punishments. They are teachers. They quiet the noise around the heart and reveal what is hidden within it. They train the soul to stand before God without leaning on every passing consolation.

Be careful whose counsel you receive. Seek wisdom from those who have walked the road, not merely from those who speak well about it. Experience is not the same as curiosity. The one who has remained, endured, and learned patiently can often see what a clever mind cannot.

When your path is peaceful for a long time, be watchful. The road to the kingdom is not usually smooth. As the soul draws nearer to God, it may meet greater struggles, not because God is absent, but because a greater gift is being prepared within it.

So let both joy and fear dwell rightly in you: joy, because hardship may be a sign that you are walking the path of the saints; fear, lest pride turn the gift into danger. But do not let fear overcome trust. Grace is nearer than the trial, and the God who permits the struggle also gives the strength to pass through it in mercy.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** *Lead me through hardship*

> **Exhale:** *Keep me in mercy*
