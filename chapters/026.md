## 26 — Choose the Better Part

> *There are many good works, but not every good work is yours to do.*

Love is generous, and because it is generous it often wants to answer every need it encounters. Yet wisdom asks another question: *What has God entrusted to me?* A calling can be lost not only through sin, but also through constant distraction. Even noble tasks may become obstacles if they draw the heart away from the work God has placed before it.

There are seasons when caring for others through practical service is the truest expression of love. There are also seasons when the greatest gift you can offer the world is a heart that remains deeply rooted in God. Prayer, silence, and contemplation are not escapes from responsibility. They are the hidden place where love is purified, strengthened, and made ready for whatever God asks next.

Do not envy another person's calling. Some are called to serve through active labor, others through quiet faithfulness. Neither path is greater in itself. The question is whether you are walking the one given to you. Peace comes when we stop trying to carry every burden and instead carry the one that has been entrusted to us.

If you have been called into stillness, guard it carefully. Not because people are unworthy of your time, but because your communion with God is the well from which every true act of love must flow. A dry well cannot refresh anyone. The soul that remains close to Christ, however hidden its life may seem, quietly blesses more people than it will ever know.

Seek first to remain with God. From that place, every act of service will arise naturally, without anxiety or resentment. Then your life, whether busy or quiet, will become an offering of love rather than an attempt to prove your worth.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** *Keep my heart with You.*

> **Exhale:** *Lead me in the work that is mine.*
