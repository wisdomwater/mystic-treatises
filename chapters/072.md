## 72 — The Cross That Leads Home

> *Do not measure God's love by the absence of hardship, but by His faithful presence within it.*

Do not think that the path toward God can be walked without difficulty. If you give yourself to righteousness, troubles will meet you along the way. Do not seek suffering, nor bring it upon yourself, but neither flee from it when it comes in the course of loving God. Receive it as a traveler receives rain on a long journey—not as proof that he has lost the road, but as part of the road itself. Every trial that reaches you passes first through the hands of divine wisdom.

When affliction comes, give thanks rather than despair. You have been counted worthy to share, in some small measure, the path walked by the prophets, the apostles, and all the saints. This is not because God delights in your pain, but because He desires to shape your heart for His kingdom. Even the willingness to endure for Christ is itself a gift of grace. Thank Him not only for the moments when faith is easy, but also for the strength He quietly gives when faith becomes costly.

Do not rejoice too greatly when your days are peaceful, nor lose heart when they are filled with sorrow. The way of God has always been marked by the cross before it opens into resurrection. If you expect another road, you will always be disappointed. But if you accept this road with trust, you will discover that no burden is carried alone. The Lord walks beside those who suffer for love, guarding them even when they cannot feel His nearness.

Know this as well: every temptation endured with humility becomes a doorway into deeper life. The soul is not weakened by trials but strengthened through them. Patience teaches what comfort never can. A heart that remains fixed on God in the midst of uncertainty grows quiet with a peace that the world cannot give or take away.

So do not fear the days that test you. Lift your eyes beyond the present struggle to the hope that has already begun within you. The God who calls you is also the God who keeps you. He will not waste your tears, nor forget your faithfulness. Every cross carried in love leads, at last, into the joy of His everlasting mercy.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** *Lead me through the cross.*

> **Exhale:** *Into Your everlasting joy.*
