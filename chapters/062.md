## 62 — Greater Than Justice

> *The mercy of God is deeper than the justice we imagine.*

Do not think of God as one who carefully measures what you deserve. If you wish to know His heart, look to Christ. See Him welcoming the laborer who came at the final hour with the same generosity as the one who bore the burden of the day. See Him running to embrace the child who squandered everything before a single word of repayment could be spoken. See Him giving His own life for sinners while they were still far from Him. If this is how He has revealed Himself, then let your heart trust His goodness without fear.

God does not become more loving or less compassionate. His mercy does not rise and fall as ours does. What He is today, He has always been, and what He has always been, He will be forever. Do not fear Him because others have painted Him as harsh. Stand in reverent awe before His love instead. Love Him, not merely because of the gifts He has placed into your hands, but because He has given you life itself. Before you could seek Him, He had already desired your existence. Before you could remember His name, His love was already calling yours.

Consider the wonder of your own life. From dust He called you into being. He breathed understanding into what could not think, and life into what could not move. Even after we have wandered from Him, He does not cease to seek us. His desire is not to abandon His creation but to restore it. His compassion reaches farther than your failures, and His purpose for you is greater than your deepest shame.

What power can overcome such love? What darkness can extinguish the grace that raises the dead and clothes what is perishable with life that cannot fade? If He has answered our rebellion with the promise of resurrection, then His mercy is greater than all our sin. Let your heart become quiet before such goodness. There comes a moment when words fail and only wonder remains. Rest there. Give thanks if you can, and if gratitude itself falls silent, know that His love remains no less complete. His mercy has held you from the beginning, and it will not let you go.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** Your mercy is without end.

> **Exhale:** I rest in Your love.
