## 43 — The Eyes of a Pure Heart

> *A heart made pure no longer searches for evil, but delights in the image of God within every person.*

Do not measure purity by extraordinary experiences or hidden revelations. The clearest sign is far simpler. Ask yourself whether your heart has begun to look upon others with mercy. When you no longer rush to condemn, when you cease delighting in another's faults, when every person appears to you as someone beloved by God, then your heart is beginning to taste true purity.

The impure heart is always collecting reasons to judge. It remembers every wound, rehearses every offense, and keeps alive the knowledge of evil. But the purified heart remembers differently. It is not blind to brokenness, yet neither is it imprisoned by it. It has learned to see beyond sin to the person whom God longs to restore.

Become, then, like a little child before God. Lay aside the cleverness that constantly weighs, compares, and accuses. There is a simplicity that is not ignorance but holiness. It is the freedom to rest in God without suspicion and to receive the world without grasping. Such simplicity is not weakness. It is the quiet strength of a heart that has been healed.

Do not lose courage if this seems beyond you. No one makes their own heart pure. This is the gentle work of grace, unfolding day by day as you remain in prayer, practice mercy, and return again to God's presence. Trust Him to cleanse your sight until you can look upon every person—and upon yourself—with the compassion that flows from His own heart.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** *Purify my heart.*

> **Exhale:** *Teach me to see with love.*
