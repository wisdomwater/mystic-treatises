## 53 — Guard the Quiet Place

> *The heart learns to love the world most deeply when it first learns to be alone with God.*

Do not think that every interruption is an act of love. There are times when the greatest kindness you can offer the world is to remain where God has called you. If the Lord is drawing you into silence, do not quickly abandon that gift because every worthy cause asks for your attention. The soul that never protects its solitude will soon have little left to give.

This does not mean you love others less. It means you are learning to love them from a deeper place. The one who treasures stillness does not flee people because they are burdens. He withdraws because he has discovered that the presence of God is the source from which all true compassion flows. When the heart drinks deeply from that living water, it returns to others with gentleness instead of exhaustion.

Do not be troubled if others misunderstand your desire for quiet. Some will think you are distant. Others may believe you have become indifferent. Let your heart remain free from the need to defend itself. God knows the love that is hidden within silence. He sees the prayers that no one hears and the tears that no one witnesses. Nothing offered to Him in secret is ever wasted.

Guard, then, the quiet place with humility. Do not cling to solitude because you despise the world, but because you long to love God with an undivided heart. As your love for Him grows, your love for others will become purer, freer, and less entangled with the need for approval or recognition.

When you withdraw into the secret place, you are not leaving love behind. You are returning to its source. There the Lord enlarges the heart until it can embrace others without losing itself. Whoever learns to dwell with God in silence will discover that every act of genuine love begins there.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** *Draw me into stillness*

> **Exhale:** *Fill me with Your love*
