## 42 — The Gift of Holy Tears

> *The tears that come from God do not empty the soul—they fill it with life.*

Do not seek tears for their own sake, but do not fear them when they come. There are tears born from sorrow over sin, and they are a mercy. They soften the hard places within you and awaken your heart from its sleep. Though they may leave you weary, they loosen the grip of everything that keeps you far from God. Receive them without shame, for they are the beginning of healing.

Yet there are other tears that are greater still. They are not drawn out by effort, nor forced by emotion. They rise quietly from a heart that has begun to behold the goodness of God. These tears are gentle and full of peace. They refresh the soul like living water and leave joy where grief once lived. They come as a gift, not as an achievement.

Do not hurry toward this gift. Remain faithful in prayer, cherish stillness, and let your heart become accustomed to the presence of God. As your love for Him deepens, your attachment to the passing things of this world will loosen of itself. The soul cannot gaze steadily upon divine beauty while clinging tightly to every earthly distraction. Little by little, your heart will learn a new homeland.

When this hidden work begins, you will discover that God is not asking you merely to become better, but to become alive. He will give you a new heart and awaken within you a wisdom that the world cannot offer. Whether your eyes are filled with tears of repentance or tears of joy, trust them both to His mercy. Each one carries you a little farther into the love that has always been seeking you.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** *Give me a new heart.*

> **Exhale:** *Lead me into Your love.*
