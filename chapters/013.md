## 13 — Remember God Before the Storm

> *The heart that remembers God in peace will know where to turn in trouble.*

Do not wait for suffering before you begin to seek God. Remember Him while your days are calm, and your heart will already know the way when darkness comes. Temptations are never far from us. They arise from within, from around us, and from circumstances we cannot foresee. Yet this is not because God has abandoned us. Rather, He has allowed this fragile life to become a school where we learn to return to Him again and again. Every trial becomes an invitation to knock upon His door.

Do not despise these hardships, for through them your soul is awakened. If life never trembled beneath your feet, how easily you would forget the One who sustains you. Fear teaches you to pray. Need teaches you to trust. Deliverance teaches you gratitude. Little by little, you begin to recognize that God has never been absent. He has been preserving you through dangers you saw and through countless others you never knew existed. In remembering His faithfulness, love for Him quietly grows.

Do not think that this passing life is your final home. God has prepared two worlds for you. This one is your place of learning, where faith is formed through freedom, weakness, repentance, and love. The world to come is your Father's house, where all that has been planted here will blossom into fullness. Because you are free to choose, your love can become real. Because you are able to wander, your return can become joyful. Even your struggles are woven into the wisdom of God, who is patiently shaping you for eternal life.

So remain near to Him while the sun is shining. Speak with Him often. Sit quietly before His presence. Let His name become familiar upon your lips and precious within your heart. Then, when sorrow visits, you will not approach Him as a stranger but as a beloved child returning home. His desire has never been your destruction, but your life. His hands remain outstretched. Turn toward Him whenever you have wandered, and you will discover that His mercy has been waiting for you long before you began your journey back.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** *I remember You, Lord.*

> **Exhale:** *Keep me near Your heart.*
