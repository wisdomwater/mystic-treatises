## 39 — The Inn of This World

> *The soul becomes wise when it remembers that this life is a night’s lodging, not its home.*

Seek wisdom with your whole strength. Bind your wandering mind with the desire for the teachings of life. There is no stronger chain against evil thoughts than the steady search for God. If you know that your earthly life has a limit, then you can also begin to set a limit to your sins.

Learn to see the bitterness hidden beneath the sweetness of the world. Many have entered this life as travelers entering an inn for a single night: kings, rulers, scholars, speakers, judges, soldiers, the wealthy, the honored, the beloved. Where are their crowns now? Where are their praises, their pleasures, their beauty, their strength, their clever words? They have passed on, and soon you also will pass.

Let this remembrance awaken you, not into despair, but into truth. The world seems beautiful while you are tangled in its nets. But when love for your soul begins to stir, you start to see its cords. What once appeared glorious becomes fragile. What once seemed worth chasing begins to feel like a prison. Tears may come then, but they are sweet tears, because they wash the heart awake.

Turn next to the Scriptures, and let them complete what nature has begun. Nature can teach you that life is brief, but Scripture reveals the hope beyond it. There the eye of the spirit is enlightened. There you learn of resurrection, mercy, judgment, promise, and the life prepared for those who seek God. Then sorrow gives way to joy, because the soul has found a hope stronger than death.

Do not fear the exchange God asks of you. What you leave is brief; what He gives is without end. The days of this life are like one drop beside the sea. Therefore, begin again with courage. Prepare provisions for the world to come: repentance, mercy, prayer, humility, and love. The road may be narrow, but it leads home.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** *Awaken my soul.*

> **Exhale:** *Lead me toward home.*
