## 87 — The Hidden Way of Humility

> *The soul that rests in God no longer seeks to be noticed, but only to remain near its Lord.*

Do not think that freedom from temptation means you no longer notice the movements of the heart. Freedom comes when those movements no longer rule you. As your love for God deepens, your mind becomes occupied with what is good, beautiful, and true. When an old temptation appears, it finds no welcome. The heart has discovered a greater delight, and so it quietly turns away. Victory is not found in never being tempted, but in no longer wishing to linger there.

Guard humility above every other virtue. Pride scatters the soul, sending it chasing after countless thoughts, ambitions, and distractions. Humility gathers the heart back into itself, where it rests before God in peace. It does not hunger to be admired, understood, or remembered. It is content to remain hidden, just as the soul itself is hidden within the body. The humble person does not seek a place of honor but gladly chooses obscurity if it allows the heart to remain undivided before the Lord.

For this reason, learn to love simplicity. Do not fill your days with more cares than love requires. Many occupations give birth to many anxieties, and many anxieties divide the heart. Keep what is necessary, but do not burden yourself with what only distracts. Protect the quiet place within you. There the soul becomes gentle, and there humility quietly grows.

You will know humility by its fruit. It does not become agitated by praise or crushed by blame. It is patient, peaceful, and slow to anger. Mercy flows easily from it. Tears come without shame. It prefers silence to argument, listening to speaking, and kindness to being right. Such a heart cannot be shaken easily, for it has ceased resting upon itself and has begun to rest in God alone.

When you come before God in prayer, do not feel compelled to say many things. There are moments when the holiest prayer is simply to bow your heart before him in quiet trust. Leave behind every claim, every achievement, and every demand. Stand before him with empty hands and whisper only this: "According to your will, my Lord." Such silence is not the absence of prayer. It is prayer brought to its deepest fulfillment, where love rests in the presence of Love itself.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** *Gather my heart to You.*

> **Exhale:** *Your will is enough.*
