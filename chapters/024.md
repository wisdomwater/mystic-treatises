## 24 — Keep Your Heart Awake

> *The deepest watchfulness is not of the eyes, but of the heart.*

Do not think that your labor for God is measured by its outward appearance. Long prayers, many words, and difficult disciplines bear little fruit if your heart wanders while your body remains. Whatever you offer to God, offer it with your whole attention. Better a few moments of watchful love than many hours spent absent in spirit. The purpose of every discipline is to awaken the heart, not merely to weary the body.

Guard what God gives you in the quiet hours. If you have tasted peace in prayer, do not scatter it carelessly through needless distractions. The warmth born in solitude is easily cooled when the mind runs after many things. What you receive in stillness, preserve through simplicity. Let your thoughts return often to God throughout the day, and the flame kindled in prayer will continue to burn quietly within you.

If, however, you pass through a season of heaviness, do not surrender to discouragement. There are times when prayer feels dry, your strength is gone, and your soul seems unable to rise. Do not abandon your place before God. If you cannot pray with many words, sit quietly before Him. If your lips grow silent, let your heart continue to wait. Refuse only one thing: the temptation to give up. Often, just beyond that season of weariness, grace returns with unexpected sweetness, and the soul is strengthened more deeply than before.

Learn, too, to welcome every change in your spiritual life with humility. Consolation and dryness, strength and weakness, fervor and silence all become teachers when they lead you back to dependence upon God. Do not trust yesterday's experience or tomorrow's hopes. Receive today's grace. The Lord knows exactly what your soul needs, and He gives each season for your healing.

Remain faithful, then. Keep your heart awake even when your feelings sleep. Continue to seek God with simplicity, and He will quietly open your eyes to treasures that cannot be found by restless striving. The soul that patiently waits before Him will discover that His presence has been sustaining it all along.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** *Keep my heart awake.*

> **Exhale:** *I wait for You, Lord.*
