## 66 — Guard the Door of Your Heart

> *What you welcome into your heart will quietly shape the person you become.*

The thoughts you allow to remain with you are never without consequence. Every remembrance leaves its imprint upon the heart. When you keep company with what is holy, your soul is gently drawn toward holiness. When you dwell upon what is bitter, impure, or vain, those same thoughts begin to fashion your desires after their own likeness. Therefore, watch carefully over the door of your heart. Not every thought deserves your hospitality.

Do not judge a word by its eloquence, but by the life from which it was born. A simple soul who has learned God through prayer may speak a single sentence that carries more life than the finest arguments of the learned. Truth spoken from experience bears a quiet authority. Receive such words with gratitude, for even the smallest spring can feed a mighty river, and God often chooses humble vessels through whom His wisdom flows.

Keep before your mind the lives of those whose love for God awakens your own. Let their faith, their patience, and their humility become familiar companions. As you remember them, your heart will be stirred toward the same beauty. The holy angels gladly strengthen such desires, encouraging the soul toward courage and perseverance. But the enemy also seeks entrance through the imagination, using old memories, fearful images, and unholy desires to weaken your resolve. Learn, then, to distinguish between the thoughts that kindle peace and those that leave the soul restless. Welcome the first. Gently dismiss the second before they take root.

Above all, let your love for God become deeper than every outward support. Love that depends only on circumstances rises and falls like a stream fed by passing rain. But love whose source is God Himself becomes a spring that never runs dry. Return often to that hidden fountain through prayer. There the heart grows quiet, the mind becomes clear, and your love no longer depends upon changing feelings or favorable days. What begins as careful watchfulness ends in joyful freedom, for the God who guards your heart is Himself the unfailing source of love.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** Guard my heart, O Lord.

> **Exhale:** Fill me with Your love.
