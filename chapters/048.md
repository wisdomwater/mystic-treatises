## 48 — The Victory That Is Given

> *Do not wound the gift of God by imagining it was your own strength that carried you.*

Do not think that you stand by your own power. When you endure temptation, when you remain faithful in sorrow, when you pass through fire and are not consumed, know this: another strength has been near you. The Lord has fought within you, and afterward, in His kindness, He has allowed your name to be called victorious.

So do not boast in your endurance. Do not look upon your labors as though they were possessions earned by your own hand. Even the desire to serve God is a gift. Even the steadiness of your heart is grace. Even the tears that cleanse you were placed in you by mercy.

Many have fallen after great victories because they forgot the One who had upheld them. They looked at their purity, their prayer, their fasting, their courage, and thought, “This is mine.” Then the hidden strength was withdrawn for a moment, and they learned how weak the soul is when it stands alone.

Therefore, tremble gently before the gift, but do not be afraid. Let gratitude guard you. When you remember that all good things come from God, humility becomes a wall around the heart. You will not need to despise yourself. You will only need to confess the truth: “Without You, I cannot stand. With You, I can pass through anything.”

Ask always for the same mercy that has carried you until now. Ask for the power that strengthens the weary, cools the fire, wipes the sweat from the face of the struggler, and turns the hour of suffering into a place of hidden consolation. The Lord is nearer than the pain. His compassion is closer than the wound. And He who gives the victory will not abandon the one who gives Him thanks.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** *You are my strength*

> **Exhale:** *Keep me in mercy*
