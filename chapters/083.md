## 83 — The Quiet Signs of Grace

> *The surest sign of God's nearness is a heart that overflows with mercy.*

As you continue faithfully in prayer, learn to watch for the quiet movements of grace within your soul. There comes a time when you begin to notice God's care everywhere. You see that he has been guiding your steps long before you recognized his hand. What once appeared to be chance now reveals itself as mercy. What once seemed like delay becomes wisdom. The soul slowly awakens to the unbroken providence of God, who never ceases to watch over his creation and lovingly directs every life toward its true home.

When this vision begins to dawn, words often become unnecessary. The heart stands before God in silence, filled with wonder rather than many thoughts. Prayer itself becomes still, not because love has grown cold, but because love has become too deep for speech. Tears may come without effort. Peace settles over the soul like a gentle cloud. Do not mistake this holy stillness for idleness. There is no greater work than resting before God with an open heart. Even when your lips are silent, your whole being offers praise.

Do not seek these gifts for their sweetness alone. They remain only where humility keeps watch. If you grow careless, they quietly withdraw until your heart returns to its first love. Knowledge by itself cannot preserve them. You may understand many holy things and yet remain untouched by them. Rejoice, not because you know the language of the spiritual life, but because its reality has begun to shape your own. Experience is the true teacher, and faithful prayer is the path by which wisdom becomes your own.

Above all, examine your heart for this one unmistakable sign. Has mercy become larger within you? Do you find yourself moved with compassion for every person, without choosing who deserves your love? Does another's sorrow become your own? When your heart begins to burn with tenderness instead of judgment, then the image of your heavenly Father is becoming visible within you.

Before you lie down to sleep, fill your mind with what is beautiful. Let the Scriptures, psalms, and words of thanksgiving be your final companions. Entrust yourself to God, and leave every anxious thought in his hands. Whether you wake in tears of joy or in quiet simplicity, know that his loving care does not cease while you sleep. His grace watches over you through the night, and when morning comes, it gently calls you again into the light of his presence.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** *Fill my heart with mercy.*

> **Exhale:** *I rest in Your care.*
