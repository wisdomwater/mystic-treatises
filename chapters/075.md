## 75 — Guard the Treasure Within

> *The heart that has tasted God no longer hungers for lesser things.*

When your heart begins to delight in God, do not imagine that the journey is finished. A treasure once found must still be guarded. Watchfulness remains necessary until the very end. Just as a healthy body grows weak when neglected, so the soul slowly loses its strength if it abandons prayer, discernment, and careful attention. Continue tending the life God has planted within you. The harvest is not complete until you have entered His eternal kingdom.

Learn to desire one thing above all others: unbroken fellowship with God. Say to Him simply, "Lord, make me dead to everything that draws my heart away from You." This is not a death of bitterness but a death of freedom. The world no longer rules the one whose deepest joy is found elsewhere. To be dead to the world is not merely to withdraw from its distractions. It is to find such fullness in God that the passing pleasures of this life no longer command your heart.

When the soul is filled with heavenly sweetness, old desires lose their power. You do not overcome every temptation by wrestling with it. Many temptations simply fade when your heart discovers something more beautiful. Just as one who has feasted has no longing for spoiled food, so the soul nourished by the love of God begins to turn naturally from what once captivated it. This is not forced obedience but healed desire.

Yet remain humble and vigilant. If you neglect the life of prayer, if you become careless with your heart, the old hungers will quietly return. An empty soul easily reaches again for what cannot satisfy. Therefore remain near the fountain of living water. Drink deeply each day. Let your thoughts dwell upon what is beautiful, true, and eternal, until your whole being learns to rest there.

Remember that your life is still being written. Every day offers another opportunity to erase what leads away from love and to write what leads toward it. Nothing has yet been sealed. While you still breathe, grace continues its patient work. Live prepared, not with fear, but with hope. The Lord who began His work within you is faithful, and He desires to welcome you into His kingdom with a heart made ready by His mercy.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** *Fill my heart with You.*

> **Exhale:** *Keep me faithful to the end.*
