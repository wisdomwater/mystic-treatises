## 16 — The Wings of the Heart

> *The soul rises toward God not by force, but by quiet faithfulness.*

Do not be discouraged because your heart is still restless. A young bird cannot soar the moment it leaves the nest. It first learns to strengthen its wings. So it is with the soul. In the beginning, gather your heart again and again through prayer, the Scriptures, repentance, and every small act of faithfulness. Even if distracting thoughts return, do not despair. You are learning to fly. Be patient with yourself, for God is patient with you.

The struggle is not only against outward temptation but against the thoughts that quietly seek a place within you. It is far easier to turn your mind gently toward what is good than to wrestle with evil after it has taken root. Fill your heart with the remembrance of God before lesser things crowd it out. A lamp does not overcome darkness by arguing with it, but by shining. Let your mind dwell often upon what is pure, and many battles will end before they begin.

At first you lean upon written words. You return to the Scriptures, recite your prayers, and remember the teachings that have guided the saints. Do not think this is a small thing. God uses these holy words to shape your heart. Yet there comes a time, by His grace alone, when His Spirit begins to write His law more deeply within you. What you once repeated with your lips becomes the quiet desire of your heart. Then obedience is no longer carried as a burden but lived as a delight.

When humility ripens within you, you will know it not by visions or extraordinary experiences, but by a heart that burns with love for God. Earthly things begin to lose their hold. Prayer becomes sweet. Tears may come without effort, flowing quietly from a heart that has been touched by mercy. Receive such gifts with gratitude, but do not cling to them. Continue in the same simple faithfulness that brought you there. The Giver is always greater than His gifts.

Walk on, then, without hurry and without fear. If your heart has not yet found this peace, keep seeking. If you have tasted it, remain watchful and grateful. In every season, God is gently teaching your soul to spread its wings and rest ever more fully in His love.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** *Lift my heart to You.*

> **Exhale:** *Teach me to rest in Your love.*
