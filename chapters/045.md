## 45 — Learning to Live for the World to Come

> *The soul becomes free when its deepest thoughts begin to dwell with God.*

Guard your heart from empty distractions, not by filling every moment with activity, but by giving your mind something greater to love. If you leave your thoughts without direction, they will wander wherever the world invites them. But if you nourish them with the Scriptures, with prayer, and with quiet meditation on the goodness of God, they will gradually learn to rest where true life is found.

There are moments in stillness when the heart forgets itself. The greatness of God becomes so beautiful that worldly concerns lose their power to captivate you. You begin to wonder at His wisdom, His mercy, and the mystery of His works. The soul drinks deeply from these thoughts until it is satisfied in a way that nothing else can satisfy. This is not an escape from life, but a foretaste of the life that is to come.

Lift your eyes often beyond the passing world. Everything you now see—even the things that seem permanent—will one day give way to a new creation. God is preparing a life beyond anything your imagination can fully grasp. Do not cling too tightly to what is temporary. Receive this world with gratitude, but let your hope rest in the One who is making all things new.

As your heart learns to dwell in this hope, you will discover a quiet freedom. Fear will lose its hold. The passing nature of this life will no longer trouble you, because your treasure is no longer here alone. Even now, while your feet remain upon the earth, your heart can begin to live in the kingdom that is coming. Trust the Lord who has prepared it for you, for His wisdom is deeper than you can fathom, and His love is already drawing you home.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** *Lift my heart above.*

> **Exhale:** *Make all things new.*
