## 78 — Let Mercy Govern Your Heart

> *The surest sign that you are drawing near to God is that your heart grows gentle toward everyone.*

Let mercy become the ruler of all your thoughts and actions. Do not ask first whether another deserves your kindness. Ask instead whether your own heart resembles the heart of Christ. If someone falls into your hands—even one who has done great evil—let no harm come to him through you. If it is within your power to preserve life, to reconcile, to heal, or to rescue, do so gladly. Leave judgment to God, whose wisdom is perfect and whose patience is beyond measure.

You were not sent into the world to become an accuser. You were sent to become a peacemaker. Pray for the sinner rather than triumph over him. Pray for the righteous that they may persevere. Return kindness for cruelty, compassion for anger, and blessing for hatred. In this way you overcome evil without becoming its servant. The victory of love is greater than the victory of force.

Do not lose hope because of your own failures. Grieve over your sins, but never call them beyond the reach of God's mercy. Remember the One who prayed for those who nailed Him to the cross. Remember Peter, who denied his Lord and was restored through love. Christ did not come searching for the flawless. He came seeking the wounded. The Physician is drawn toward those who know they are sick and are willing to be healed.

Because your days are passing swiftly, do not delay what love asks of you today. Pray while your hands can still be lifted. Weep while your eyes can still shed tears. Serve while your feet can still walk the path of righteousness. Live each day as one preparing for a joyful journey home, not with fear, but with readiness. When the final summons comes, let it find you carrying nothing but faith, hope, and mercy.

Above all, remember this: the heart that learns mercy begins to see with God's own eyes. As you become compassionate toward others, a hidden light rises within your soul. That light is already the dawn of the kingdom, where mercy rejoices forever over judgment.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** *Make my heart merciful.*

> **Exhale:** *As You are merciful.*
