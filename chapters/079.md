## 79 — Better to Know Your Own Heart

> *The greatest miracle is not to see heaven, but to awaken to the truth within your own soul.*

Repentance is born in a quiet heart. If you desire its healing, do not fear solitude. Withdraw, at least for a time, from the noise that scatters your thoughts. In stillness you begin to hear the questions that matter most: How shall I come to God? How shall I enter the life that never ends? Such questions cannot be answered in the marketplace. They grow only where the heart has learned to be alone with its Lord.

Do not choose solitude because it seems noble, nor because others praise it. Choose it because you long for God. Accept the small discomforts that come with it—the inconveniences, the misunderstandings, the hidden sacrifices. These are not obstacles to the way; they are part of the way. The saints did not flee the world to escape labor. Many endured weakness, sickness, and long seasons when they could scarcely pray with their lips. Yet even their silence became prayer, and their helplessness became an offering before God.

Do not envy those whose gifts are seen by many. Better to see your own sins than to witness angels. Better to weep over your own heart than to amaze the world with wonders. Better to cling to Christ in hidden sorrow than to receive the applause of a crowd. The eye that sees its own need has already begun to see with the light of God. Such knowledge is deeper than visions, for it gives birth to humility, and humility opens the soul to grace.

Let the thought of your mortality become a faithful companion, not to fill you with fear, but to free you from illusion. This life passes quickly, but the kingdom toward which you journey is everlasting. As the love of God grows within you, the glitter of this world loses its hold. What once seemed indispensable becomes strangely small beside the hope that Christ has placed in your heart.

Walk gently, therefore, into the silence where repentance flowers. There Christ waits for you—not to condemn you, but to make you alive. As your heart learns to dwell with Him, even loneliness becomes companionship, and every quiet moment becomes a foretaste of the eternal joy prepared for those who love Him.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** *Open the eyes of my heart.*

> **Exhale:** *Lead me into Your life.*
