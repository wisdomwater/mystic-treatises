## 10 — The Habit of Light

> *What the soul practices in secret becomes the world it carries into prayer.*

Let your prayers be followed by works of excellence, so that your soul may see the flower of the light of truth. The heart is shaped by what it returns to. If you give even a little care and effort, the soul can exchange one habit for another. What once wandered among passing things can learn to dwell among the words of life.

Burden your soul, then, with holy reading, even when delight does not come at first. Read the narrow ways of humility, the stories of the saints, the mysteries of Scripture, and the providence of God across the generations. At first the mind may resist because it is still darkened by old memories. But in time, when you rise for prayer, the images of Scripture will stand before you instead of worldly thoughts.

Do not expect the fire of divine love to burn easily in a heart still loving comfort. Fresh wood does not catch flame quickly. A soul tied to many things cannot cling wholly to spiritual wisdom. Give away what is beyond your need. Speak to God with the frankness of a child before a father. Nothing brings the heart so near to God as mercy, and nothing gives the mind such peace as voluntary poverty.

When you give, do not weigh who is worthy. Let all people be worthy of your kindness. Give with a joyful face. Offer more than was asked when true need stands before you. Our Lord ate with publicans and sinners, not because He ignored truth, but because He drew them toward life through bodily kindness and spiritual mercy.

So close the gates of your senses when you can, and let the inner struggle be faced in silence. Exchange needless activity for prayer. Fall before the cross. Stretch your whole heart toward God. Then your light will spread like dawn, and your soul will become like a watered garden, a fountain that does not run dry.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** *Shape my heart by Your Word.*

> **Exhale:** *Make me a fountain of mercy.*
