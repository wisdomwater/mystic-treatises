## 84 — Grace Holds You

> *Do not despair in the storm or grow proud in the calm; both teach you to rest in God.*

Do not be surprised when the condition of your heart changes. One day prayer may feel clear and peaceful, and the next your mind may be troubled by thoughts you believed had long since passed away. This happens not only to beginners but also to those who have walked with God for many years. As the air changes from warmth to cold, from storm to stillness, so the soul passes through seasons of rest and struggle.

When temptation returns, do not imagine that all your progress has been lost. Do not give yourself over to discouragement. The spiritual life is not a place where struggle disappears forever. As long as you live in the body, you will be invited again and again to choose love, faithfulness, and trust. The struggle itself becomes a way of offering your love to God. Each time you turn toward him in weakness, your heart learns more deeply where its true strength is found.

When grace brings peace, receive it with thanksgiving, but do not claim it as your own achievement. You are not standing because you have become stronger than others. You are standing because grace is carrying you. Remember the days when your thoughts were confused, when fear or desire quickly pulled you away. Such memories are not given to shame you, but to keep your heart tender. They teach you to look upon every person with compassion, knowing how easily any soul can be troubled.

Humility is the salt of every virtue. Prayer, fasting, knowledge, and service lose their sweetness when pride enters them. Yet humility can heal even many failures. When you fall, do not hide from God. Return with a softened heart. Admit your weakness without condemning yourself, and let repentance become a doorway rather than a prison. God does not ask you to prove that you are strong. He asks you to remain open to the mercy that raises you again.

Give thanks, then, in both the storm and the calm. In peace, remember that grace sustains you. In struggle, remember that grace has not abandoned you. God permits your weakness to become visible so that your eyes may remain fixed upon him. And as you continually seek refuge in his love, faith grows steadier, hope grows stronger, and your heart learns that nothing—not even its own changing weather—can separate it from his care.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** *Your grace carries me.*

> **Exhale:** *I return in humility.*
