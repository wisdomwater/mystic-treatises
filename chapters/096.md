## 96 — Do Not Turn Back

> *Faithfulness is measured less by unbroken strength than by a heart that always returns to God.*

The saints did not become radiant because they never grew weary. They became radiant because they never stopped returning to God. Even in old age, when their bodies had grown frail and sleep pressed heavily upon them, they guarded the flame of prayer with quiet perseverance. What sustained them was not stubbornness but love. They had discovered that every hour spent before God, however difficult, slowly filled the soul with a light that no earthly comfort could give.

The work of prayer changes more than the heart. As the soul is filled with the light of Christ, even the whole person begins to reflect His peace. This is the hidden glory prepared for those who remain with Him. The saints did not seek extraordinary experiences, yet because they sought God alone, He transformed them from within. Their joy was not found in visions or gifts, but in the One whose presence became dearer to them than sleep, ease, or praise from others.

Do not imagine that you must possess the strength of the saints before you begin to walk their path. There will be seasons when your body is weak, your mind distracted, and your prayers seem small. Do not despise those days. God knows your strength better than you know it yourself. He does not ask from you what you do not have. Offer Him what you can today, and let that offering be made with gladness rather than shame. A little prayer offered with love is more pleasing than great labors carried beneath the weight of discouragement.

If you stumble, do not abandon the journey. No merchant completes every voyage without loss. No farmer gathers every seed that is planted. No soldier wins a battle without receiving wounds. Why should you expect the spiritual life to be different? Temptations will come. Failures will come. There will be days when you feel as though you have lost ground. Yet do not turn your back. Rise again. Return again. The mercy of God is greater than your weakness, and His patience is longer than your failures. The One who called you into this hidden way has no desire to cast you aside. Walk on with quiet courage, trusting that every sincere return is already embraced by His unfailing love.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** *I return to You.*

> **Exhale:** *Your mercy holds me.*
