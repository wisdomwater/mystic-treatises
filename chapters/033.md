## 33 — The Brightness Beneath the Rust

> *God does not add His light from outside; He uncovers the brightness He has already placed within you.*

When holy words become sweet to you, do not stop at the sweetness alone. Let their delight lead you toward their source. The heart is like iron covered with rust. The file does not create the brightness within the metal; it only removes what hides it. So it is with the mind. Prayer, Scripture, discipline, and faithful service do not place light in you as though God had forgotten to give it. They clear away what has gathered over the heart, until the hidden radiance begins to shine.

Do not despise the labor that prepares you for truth. Bread earned with sweat is sweet to the farmer. In the same way, the bread of divine wisdom nourishes the soul that has endured the work of seeking. Without effort, the words of life may touch the ear but fail to feed the heart. Yet when longing, patience, and humility have softened the ground, even a small word from God becomes food.

Understand also the power of what you repeatedly choose. Freedom is given to the soul, but whatever you surrender yourself to begins to shape you. If you yield again and again to what darkens the heart, that habit becomes a chain. If you yield again and again to goodness, prayer, mercy, and truth, grace forms a holy strength within you. What began as a choice becomes a path, and the path becomes the movement of your life.

For now, your knowledge of God remains partial. Do not be troubled by this. If the whole truth were placed upon the soul while it is still able to wander, the weight would overwhelm it. So God gives both trust and holy fear. Trust lifts you toward His goodness; fear keeps you humble before the mystery of His judgments. One moment you are steadied by confidence, another moment you are brought back to carefulness. Both are gifts when they keep you near to Him.

So remain patient with your incomplete understanding. Let prayer polish the heart. Let Scripture feed you. Let humility guard your steps. The God whose judgments are unsearchable is also the God whose goodness is without end. What is hidden from you now is not hidden in cruelty, but in mercy, until your heart is made ready for the fullness of His light.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** *Polish my heart, O Lord.*

> **Exhale:** *Reveal Your hidden light.*
