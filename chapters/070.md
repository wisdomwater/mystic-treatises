## 70 — The Crown Belongs to Those Who Endure

> *The strength to endure is not found in yourself, but in the love that carries you.*

Do not be discouraged when the way of God feels difficult. Love is always proved by perseverance. If people have endured hardship, poverty, ridicule, sickness, and even death for the sake of earthly honor or human wisdom, how much more should the love of God strengthen your heart. The One who calls you is not asking you to carry your burdens alone. He gives grace to those who choose to remain faithful, one step at a time.

Do not say, "I am too weak for this path." You are right—you are too weak by yourself. No one overcomes temptation or suffering by the strength of human will alone. Yet when your small willingness is joined to the help of God, trials that once seemed impossible begin to lose their power. The Lord does not ask you to become stronger than your weakness. He asks you to trust Him within it.

When you stumble, do not conclude that the journey is over. When another stands firm, do not imagine they arrived there without many hidden battles. Every saint has learned endurance through countless acts of quiet faithfulness that few ever saw. Victory belongs not to those who never struggled, but to those who continued to entrust themselves to God through every struggle. The crown is not given at the beginning of the race but at its faithful completion.

Therefore, do not measure your life by today's strength or today's failures. Lift your eyes to Christ and continue. His grace is already at work within you, preparing your heart for joys you cannot yet imagine. Every burden borne in love becomes lighter in His hands. Every act of perseverance deepens your soul. And every step you take toward Him is met by One who has been walking toward you from the beginning. Hold fast, for His love is stronger than every hardship, and His faithfulness will bring you safely home.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** Strengthen me, Lord.

> **Exhale:** I will endure in Your love.
