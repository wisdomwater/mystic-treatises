## 23 — Stay in the Quiet

> *The soul learns the voice of God in the silence it faithfully keeps.*

Do not think that constant activity will bring you closer to God. The heart that is pulled in every direction cannot remain at peace. Every unnecessary care, every restless pursuit, every distraction opens the door to thoughts that draw the soul away from its true home. If you desire to know what is happening within your own heart, make room for quiet. Stand often before God without haste, and learn to notice the smallest movements of your spirit before they grow into stronger desires.

If you have once tasted the sweetness of God's presence and later find your heart cold, do not lose hope. Ask yourself whether you have allowed too many voices to fill the silence that once belonged to Him. The soul easily forgets its first love when it is constantly occupied with lesser things. Yet the warmth can return. Humble yourself. Pray with tears if they are given to you. Return to the quiet place. The heart that thirsts again for God will discover that He has never ceased waiting there.

Guard the tenderness of your soul. A young plant is easily bruised before its roots have grown deep. In the same way, the beginnings of prayer, stillness, and holy desire require careful protection. Do not expose your heart unnecessarily to the noise, arguments, and endless concerns of the world. Solitude is not an escape from love but a place where love is purified. As the roots of a tree sink quietly beside living water, so your soul grows strong when it remains hidden with God.

Do not measure your life by how much you accomplish, but by how faithfully you remain in His presence. The world praises busyness, but heaven delights in a heart that quietly watches before the Lord. From that hidden place come discernment, peace, compassion, and strength for every good work. The soul that abides with God carries His fragrance wherever it goes.

Remain, then, in the quiet whenever you are able. Let silence become your teacher, prayer your breath, and the presence of God your greatest companionship. In stillness you will discover that the One you have been seeking has been speaking to your heart all along.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** *Quiet my heart, Lord.*

> **Exhale:** *Teach me Your stillness.*
