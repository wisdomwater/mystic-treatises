## 82 — Learn to Read the Heart

> *The truest measure of your spiritual life is the quiet condition of your heart before God.*

Do not judge your life with God by the number of your prayers, your disciplines, or the sacrifices you have made. These things are good, but they are not the deepest measure. Look instead at what is quietly taking place within your heart. Ask yourself whether love is growing stronger than fear, whether hope is becoming steadier than anxiety, whether peace remains even when trials visit you. The Great Physician heals the soul in many ways. Sometimes he uses hardship. Sometimes illness. Sometimes hidden temptations. Sometimes he visits you with tears, warmth, and an indescribable joy. Receive them all as part of his patient work of healing.

Watch your heart gently. Do not do so with suspicion, but with honesty. Notice which passions have begun to lose their strength. Notice which wounds still ache when touched. Some temptations come only from old memories. Others arrive with sudden force. Some seek to persuade you; others merely weary you. Learn to recognize them without becoming afraid of them. The one who knows the movements of the heart is less easily ruled by them. This quiet knowledge is itself a gift of grace.

As your soul grows healthier, you will discover another kind of prayer. Distractions begin to loosen their grip. At times your mind is drawn toward God without effort, as though carried by a gentle current beyond words. Joy rises unexpectedly and leaves the tongue silent. There are moments when the whole person seems filled with a peace that cannot be explained, and every earthly treasure appears small beside the sweetness of God's presence. Do not seek these consolations for their own sake, but receive them with gratitude whenever the Lord gives them.

If these gifts seem far away, do not despair. Continue your prayer. Keep your vigils. Bow your knees. Return to the Scriptures. Ask Christ to remove whatever still veils your heart. He is faithful to those who seek him with patience. What begins as labor will, in time, become delight. The witness God gives within the heart is stronger than any argument, and the soul that has tasted his loving-kindness no longer walks by hearsay, but by living experience. Remain with him, and he will gently lead you from darkness into his marvelous light.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** *Heal the hidden places.*

> **Exhale:** *Lead me into Your light.*
