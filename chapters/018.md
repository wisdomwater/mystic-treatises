## 18 — The Strength of the Weak

> *The soul discovers the strength of God only after it has ceased trusting in its own.*

Do not be ashamed of your weakness. It is often the doorway through which God draws nearest. As long as you imagine yourself strong, you will lean upon your own wisdom, your own discipline, and your own resolve. But when you discover how easily fear unsettles you, how quickly temptation finds you, and how little strength you truly possess, a deeper wisdom begins to awaken. You learn that you were never meant to carry yourself alone.

Do not think that God has abandoned you when He allows you to see your frailty. He is revealing something precious. Only the one who knows his need will seek help with his whole heart. And the one who seeks with sincerity cannot help but become humble. A heart that cries out for mercy has already begun to leave pride behind. This is why your weakness, painful though it may be, can become the beginning of every true blessing.

As your heart bows low before God, His mercy quietly surrounds you. Then a new confidence begins to rise—not confidence in yourself, but in Him. You discover that prayer is no longer merely a duty. It becomes your refuge. It is the harbor where your fears find rest, the light that shines when your path grows dark, the shelter that stands firm when temptations gather, the medicine that heals hidden wounds, and the shield that guards your heart. Prayer does not change because your circumstances become easier. It changes because you begin to know the One who hears you.

Do not grow weary, then, when your weakness is revealed. Let it lead you back to God again and again. Every sincere prayer softens the heart. Every cry for help opens it more fully to grace. Every act of humble trust makes room for the strength that is not your own.

Blessed is the one who has learned this holy secret: the heart that has nothing left to boast of is the heart that becomes full of God. Where humility takes root, mercy is never far away. Rest there without fear, for the Lord does not despise the broken heart. He fills it with His own life.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** *Lord, I need You.*

> **Exhale:** *Be my strength today.*
