## 76 — Repentance Opens the Door

> *Repentance is not the end of hope; it is the beginning of life.*

Repentance is the doorway through which life begins again. Do not think of it as sorrow alone. It is the grace that restores what has been neglected, the hand that leads you back when you have wandered, the quiet opening through which Christ welcomes you once more into His joy. Every sincere turning of the heart is already a sign that His mercy has been at work within you.

Do not be afraid if your heart begins to grieve over its own distance from God. Such sorrow is not your enemy. It is a holy gift. The Lord gently awakens the soul from its sleep until it remembers the life for which it was created. In that awakening you begin to long for what is eternal, to hunger for His kingdom, and to see the passing things of this world in their true measure. This grief is not meant to crush you. It is the fire that softens the heart so it can receive living water.

The beginning of this path is humble and quiet. Do not seek to appear spiritual before others. Seek instead a heart that bows before God without pretense. True wisdom grows in hidden places. It is nourished by silence, by honest prayer, and by a soul that no longer needs the praise of the world. As the heart grows still, it becomes more attentive to God's presence, and in that presence humility flowers almost unnoticed.

Do not measure your life by what you accomplish, but by the degree to which your heart has awakened to God. The soul that dies to vanity begins to live with a freedom it has never known. What once seemed glorious grows strangely small, while the quiet beauty of God's love becomes enough. This is the beginning of true life—not the loss of yourself, but the discovery of your deepest self in Him.

So return again today. Do not delay because you think you have wandered too far or failed too often. The gate of repentance is never locked to the one who comes with sincerity. Christ Himself waits there, not to condemn you, but to restore you, until your heart rests once more in the joy of His unfailing mercy.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** *Turn my heart to You.*

> **Exhale:** *Restore me in Your mercy.*
