## 1 — A Heart Undivided

> *The heart finds rest when it learns to love one thing above all else.*

Every journey toward God begins by turning your attention. Your heart cannot be divided forever between what passes away and what endures. As long as your thoughts are scattered among the countless distractions of the world, they will never discover the quiet in which God speaks. Do not think that holiness begins with extraordinary deeds. It begins with returning your wandering mind, again and again, to what is eternal.

Train yourself to linger over the words that give life. Let them become familiar companions until they shape the way you think, the way you hope, and the way you endure suffering. Patience grows from this kind of meditation, and meditation is strengthened by patience. Together they become the foundation upon which every virtue is built. A house cannot stand without a foundation, nor can the soul become whole without this faithful rhythm of returning to God.

Do not imagine that drawing near to God requires you to flee from the world with your body. What must be left behind is the tyranny of the world within you. It is possible to live among people while your heart rests quietly in God, just as it is possible to dwell in the wilderness while remaining captive to restless thoughts. Freedom is not found in a place but in a heart that is no longer ruled by every passing desire.

When your love for God becomes stronger than your love for yourself, fear begins to lose its voice. Troubles that once seemed unbearable become lighter because they are carried in hope. Even suffering becomes a teacher, revealing that God has never abandoned you to chance. As your trust deepens, you will discover that the Scriptures are no longer merely words upon a page. They become living waters, drawing your heart ever deeper into mysteries that no worldly pleasure can satisfy.

Walk this path with simplicity. Return your thoughts whenever they wander. Feed your soul with what is true. In time, you will find that your deepest longing has not been to escape the world, but to awaken to the presence of God that has been waiting for you all along.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** Gather my wandering heart.

> **Exhale:** Lead me into Your rest.
