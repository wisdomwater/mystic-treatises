## 97 — Solitude That Learns to Love

> *The truest solitude is not separation from people, but a heart made spacious enough to love them all.*

Do not imagine that solitude and mercy stand against one another. They are companions when they are rightly understood. I do not seek quiet because I love people less, but because I desire to love them more deeply. In stillness the heart is purified from its restlessness and learns to remain with God. There, hidden from noise and distraction, love grows without seeking recognition. If your solitude does not make you more merciful, then it has not yet become the solitude that God desires.

Carry every person in your heart before you carry them with your hands. Pray for all. Let compassion become the quiet atmosphere of your soul. Then, when the moment comes to serve, your outward actions will simply reveal what God has already formed within you. Never allow the pursuit of silence to become an excuse for withholding love. Whenever your brother or sister stands before you in genuine need, Christ Himself has drawn near. Do not hide behind spiritual practices if love is asking something of you.

Do not compare yourself with those whose calling is different from your own. Some were led into such profound solitude that they rarely saw another human face. Do not use their example to excuse yourself from the opportunities for mercy God has placed directly before you. Walk faithfully according to the measure He has given you today. If someone who is weary, sick, lonely, or hungry comes within your reach, receive that interruption as a gift. Love fulfilled in a simple act is greater than solitude protected by indifference.

Remember the holy ones who gladly laid aside their own plans to care for another. They did not lose God by serving; they found Him more deeply. Love of God and love of neighbor are not two separate roads but one path with two footsteps. The more deeply you enter the presence of God, the more naturally His compassion begins to flow through you. Therefore seek silence with all your heart, but let that silence become generous. Seek prayer, but let prayer teach your hands to serve. Then your life will become a living reflection of the Father, whose heart is both perfectly still and endlessly full of mercy.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** *Fill my heart with Your love.*

> **Exhale:** *Let that love overflow.*
