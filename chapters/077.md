## 77 — The Light Born from Silence

> *At first you enter silence with effort; then silence begins to draw you toward God.*

Do not weaken yourself through harshness, as though exhaustion were holiness. Measure your discipline with wisdom. The body is a servant of prayer, not an enemy to be destroyed. Give it what is necessary, but do not allow comfort to dull the heart. Walk the middle path with humility, asking Christ to guard you from both carelessness and pride.

When a thought returns again and again, do not immediately obey it or cast it aside. Bring it into prayer. Some desires appear beautiful but are not suited to your strength, your season, or the work God has given you. Say, “Lord, let Your truth dawn within me. If this desire comes from You, establish it. If it is a hidden snare, let it fade.” Remain patient. What is born of God becomes clearer in humility and peace.

Above all, learn to love silence. At first you may need to compel yourself to be still. The tongue will search for words, and the mind will wander through many rooms. Remain quietly before God. In time, something hidden will be born within you—a sweetness that no speech can express. Then silence will no longer feel empty. It will become a doorway through which the heart enters the presence of Christ.

Do not interrupt prayer merely because words have ceased. When the mind becomes still, let it rest. When tears come, do not imagine that you have abandoned your prayer. Tears may be the prayer’s fulfillment. When kneeling becomes peaceful, do not hurry to rise. God is not measuring the number of sentences you have spoken. He receives the heart that remains before Him in simplicity.

Yet remember that silence must ripen into mercy. A quiet heart that becomes hard has not yet learned the silence of God. Let compassion outweigh every discipline. Mercy clears the mist from your own soul and reveals the One whose image you bear. Be gentle with the weakness of others, as you ask God to be gentle with yours. Then your silence will not separate you from the world but fill you with love for it.

Remain still, therefore, and let grace teach you what words cannot. Beneath the noise of your thoughts, the mercy of God is already waiting.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** *Draw me into silence.*

> **Exhale:** *Fill me with Your mercy.*
