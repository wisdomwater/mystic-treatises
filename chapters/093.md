## 93 — Do Not Count the Milestones

> *Prayer is not measured by how much you accomplish, but by how deeply you remain with God.*

When you rise in the night to pray, do not hurry into words. First become still. Let your body quiet itself, and let your heart remember whose presence you are entering. Ask the Lord to strengthen your weakness, to guard your mind from restless thoughts, and to purify your heart from every affection that would cloud your love. Say to Him with simplicity, "You know my frailty better than I do. Keep me near You." Such a prayer is already the beginning of worship. When the heart bows in humility, grace often opens it before a single psalm has been spoken.

Do not make your labor into a burden by measuring it. If dawn approaches before you have finished everything you intended, leave something undone with peace rather than allowing anxiety to steal the sweetness of prayer. When the thought whispers, "Hurry. Do more. Finish everything," do not believe it. I do not seek to count milestones; I seek to enter the dwelling place of God. Better to pray one psalm with an undivided heart than many while your soul is scattered. Love is never nourished by haste.

If weariness overtakes you, do not surrender to discouragement. If you cannot stand, then sit. If you cannot continue reading, let your tongue become silent while your heart remains awake before God. Wakefulness itself is a gift. There are many faithful ways to keep vigil. Some pray with psalms. Others with tears. Some bow low before the Lord, while others quietly repeat a single prayer through the long hours of the night. God does not weigh the form of your offering as much as the love with which it is given.

There are moments when prayer becomes so full of quiet joy that time itself seems to disappear. The soul forgets its weariness, and even the longing for sleep fades before the sweetness of God's presence. Scripture, prayer, silence, and remembrance begin to flow together like living water. The mind turns away from every distraction and rests alone with its Beloved. Seek this communion above every spiritual achievement. Remain with God in the hidden chamber of your heart, and let love, not accomplishment, become the measure of your prayer. There, in stillness, He will gently reveal Himself, and the long night will begin to shine with the light of His coming.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** *Keep my heart with You.*

> **Exhale:** *Teach me the stillness of love.*
