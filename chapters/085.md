## 85 — Do Not Grow Weary in Prayer

> *As long as the heart can change, it must continually return to God.*

Do not imagine that repentance belongs only to the beginning of the spiritual life. You need it at every hour. Repentance is not endless self-accusation, but the humble movement of the heart toward God: asking mercy for what has passed and grace for what lies ahead. It is the soul refusing to live apart from divine help.

For this reason, watch and pray. Ask, seek, and knock without growing tired. The Lord himself has invited you to come repeatedly, even in the middle of the night, even when your need feels embarrassing, even when the door seems slow to open. His command to ask is already a promise of welcome. He knows what will heal you, and he delights to give what leads you toward life.

Do not be surprised that temptation remains, even after years of prayer. There is no place of complete safety in this world where vigilance is no longer needed. The heart may be peaceful today and troubled tomorrow. Even great grace does not remove the need for dependence. Sometimes God allows weakness to remain so that love may deepen, pride may soften, and the soul may continue to seek refuge in him.

When you feel your weakness, remember the word given to Paul: grace is sufficient, and divine strength is revealed most clearly where human strength fails. Your trials are not proof that God has abandoned you. They may become the very means by which his presence guards you. What you would remove, he may use to keep your heart awake, humble, and close to him.

Therefore, do not surrender to discouragement. You may fall many times and still rise once more. The one who has often been thrown down may yet finish the race with courage. Do not neglect prayer. Do not stop asking for help. The Lord does not measure you only by how often you stumble, but by the heart that continues to turn toward him. As long as you return, mercy is already meeting you on the way.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** *Your grace is enough.*

> **Exhale:** *I return to You.*
