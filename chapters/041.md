## 41 — The Hidden Fruit of Stillness

> *When the noise around you grows quiet, the voice within you can finally be heard.*

Do not think of fasting as a burden placed upon your life, but as a door opened toward God. When you willingly set aside what satisfies the body, you make room for a deeper hunger to awaken. Prayer begins to rise more naturally. The heart becomes watchful. Even weakness can become a friend if it teaches you to lean upon God instead of yourself. Better to grow weary through love than dull through comfort.

Yet do not imagine that outward discipline alone can bring peace to your soul. Fasting, vigils, and every good labor protect you and strengthen your resolve, but they cannot by themselves quiet the hidden movements of the heart. There are thoughts that only silence can uncover, wounds that only stillness can reveal, and desires that only the light of God can heal. If your life is filled without ceasing by noise, activity, and constant distraction, how will you ever discover what lies within you?

Learn, then, to love holy solitude. Withdraw, even for a little while, from the endless voices that surround you. Let your senses grow quiet. Let your mind return from its wandering. In that peaceful place your heart will begin to examine itself with honesty and without fear. What was once hidden will slowly come into the light, not to condemn you, but to be cleansed. As muddy water becomes clear when it is left undisturbed, so your soul becomes transparent when it rests before God.

Do not become discouraged if this work is slow. Purity is not gained in a single day, but through patient faithfulness. As you remain in prayer, the Lord will gently reveal a wisdom that has long been waiting beneath the surface of your life. And when the heart begins to soften, tears may come—not tears of despair, but the quiet gift of grace. Receive them with gratitude. They are a sign that the Spirit is drawing you ever deeper into the mercy of God, where true peace is found.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** *Quiet my heart.*

> **Exhale:** *Lead me into Your peace.*
