## 95 — The Light of the Night

> *The soul that keeps watch with God begins to taste the dawn before the sun has risen.*

There is a quiet gift hidden in the hours of the night. When the world grows silent and every voice is stilled, the heart can more easily hear the whisper of God. This is why our Lord often withdrew into lonely places to pray. He did not merely command us to watch and pray—He showed us the way with His own life. Seek moments of holy stillness, whether in the night or in any hour of solitude, and you will discover that prayer is not simply something you do. It becomes the place where your soul learns to dwell with God.

Guard this communion with care. Do not burden your body beyond wisdom, nor indulge it until it becomes dull. Whatever clouds the mind also clouds prayer. The heart longs to stand before God with openness and simplicity, free from needless distractions. Every small act of watchfulness prepares the soul to receive His light. Little by little, the inner life grows clearer, and what once seemed hidden begins to shine with quiet understanding.

Do not be surprised if prayer is resisted. Whenever your heart turns deeply toward God, there are many things that will seek to pull it away. Weariness, wandering thoughts, discouragement, and the sweetness of comfort will all whisper that you can pray another time. Do not fear these struggles. They are not signs that God has abandoned you, but reminders that your attention is precious. Even when your prayer feels poor or distracted, remain faithful. No sincere turning toward God is ever wasted. Every moment you offer Him becomes a seed that He knows how to bring to life.

Those who persevere in prayer discover that it becomes a light within them. Their minds grow clear. Their hearts become gentle. Hope rises where fear once lived, and the soul begins to glimpse the life that is still to come. Prayer renews the spirit, strengthens love, and lifts the eyes beyond the passing world. This is why the saints treasured it above every other labor. They had learned that to remain with God is already to taste eternity. Keep watch, then, not because God demands it, but because He delights to meet those who quietly wait for Him. In His presence, the longest night is already becoming morning.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** *I wait for You, Lord.*

> **Exhale:** *Fill me with Your light.*
