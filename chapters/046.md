## 46 — When Prayer Becomes Your Breath

> *The deepest prayer is no longer something you do, but a life you receive.*

Do not measure your prayer only by the moments you have set aside each day. These times are holy, but they are meant to awaken something that continues long after you have risen from your knees. If you remain faithful in seeking God, there may come a quiet change within you. The remembrance of Him will no longer need to be forced. Like a spring hidden beneath the earth, prayer will begin to rise of itself.

This is not the work of human effort alone. No one can manufacture such a gift. The Holy Spirit gently teaches the heart to abide with God until every part of life becomes touched by His presence. Whether you are working or resting, speaking or silent, awake or asleep, the soul quietly turns toward its Beloved. Even silence becomes a prayer, for the heart has learned to rest before the One it loves.

Do not seek extraordinary experiences. Seek purity, humility, and obedience. Give yourself to the simple work of prayer, to the Scriptures, to mercy, and to watchfulness over your heart. Leave the hidden gifts to God. He knows when the soul is ready to receive them, and He gives them not as a reward for achievement but as an expression of His grace.

The surest sign that prayer has taken root is not that you speak more words, but that love awakens more quickly. The mere remembrance of God warms your heart. His name becomes dear to you. Tears may come without striving, not from sorrow alone, but from love. This is the beginning of heaven within the soul. Walk patiently toward it, trusting that the Spirit Himself is praying within you long before you know how to pray.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** *Remain with me.*

> **Exhale:** *Pray within me.*
