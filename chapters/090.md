## 90 — Carry the Cross Before You

> *Do not seek the path with the least hardship, but the one that leads you most surely to Christ.*

Do not be surprised when difficulties arise as soon as you begin to follow the Lord with greater earnestness. Do not think that resistance means you have chosen the wrong path. Every true virtue carries a cross, for it asks you to leave behind the life that seeks comfort above all else. If you would walk with Christ, learn to expect labor without losing heart. The cross is not an interruption to the journey. It is the road itself.

Before you begin any good work, settle this within your heart: there will be effort, there will be sacrifice, and there will be moments when you are tempted to turn back. If you accept this beforehand, many fears lose their power. The soul that has already embraced hardship cannot easily be frightened by it. But the soul that constantly searches for ease is troubled even by small obstacles, for disappointment is born wherever comfort is expected as a right.

Look at the world around you. How willingly people endure long labor, danger, and sleepless nights for wealth that fades, honor that passes away, or pleasures that cannot satisfy the heart. They cross stormy seas and walk difficult roads without asking whether the journey will be easy. Shall the one who seeks the kingdom of God expect a gentler road than those who seek passing things?

Let your zeal be spiritual rather than restless. When love for Christ fills the heart, courage quietly follows. The body may still grow weary, and trials may still come, but the soul is no longer ruled by fear. It stands firm because its hope is fixed on something greater than comfort. Holy zeal does not make suffering pleasant; it makes love stronger than suffering. From this strength are born endurance, steadfastness, and the quiet resolve that refuses to abandon the way.

Therefore, do not measure your progress by how comfortable the journey feels. Measure it by your willingness to remain faithful. Each burden carried in love becomes lighter than it first appeared, because Christ bears it with you. Walk forward without fear. The cross that seems heavy today is leading you toward the life that cannot be taken away, and the One who called you onto this path will never leave you to walk it alone.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** *Strengthen my resolve.*

> **Exhale:** *I follow Your way.*
