## 52 — The Strength Hidden in Humility

> *God does not always remove the burden. Often He strengthens the one who carries it.*

Not every trial comes for the same reason. Some hardships are given by a loving Father to deepen your heart. They humble you, loosen your grip on yourself, and teach you to seek help that only God can give. In these trials, light and darkness often mingle together. You may feel weak, yet you are quietly being made strong. You may know sorrow, yet beneath it grace is enlarging your soul.

When suffering comes, do not measure God's love by the weight of the burden. Measure it by His faithfulness within it. The Lord knows the strength of each soul and never ceases to watch over those who entrust themselves to Him. Even when you cannot see His hand, He is training your heart for a greater share in His life.

If you find yourself overwhelmed, do not harden your heart or search everywhere for relief. Go first to humility. Many troubles become heavier because we insist on carrying them by our own strength. Humility opens the heart to receive what pride cannot hold. It teaches the soul to pray, to weep, and to wait for mercy instead of demanding escape.

As humility grows, endurance is born. As endurance grows, the burden becomes lighter. As the burden grows lighter, love for God deepens. And where love deepens, joy quietly begins to blossom, even before the trial has passed. This is the hidden work of grace, changing suffering into communion.

Do not ask only that your troubles be taken away. Ask that they bear their good fruit. Our compassionate Father often leaves the trial for a season, not because He has forgotten His children, but because He desires to perfect them through it. He never wastes a sorrow that is entrusted to Him. The soul that walks with Him through hardship will discover that mercy has been carrying it all along.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** *Strengthen me in trial*

> **Exhale:** *Perfect me in love*
