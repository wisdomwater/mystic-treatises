## 56 — The Harbor Toward Which You Sail

> *Let prayer be the star that guides your heart across the sea of this world.*

Do not think that your life has no direction simply because the journey is long. A sailor does not despair because he has not yet reached the shore. He keeps his eyes upon the stars and steadily guides his ship toward the harbor. So let prayer become the fixed light by which you order your days. Return to it again and again, and it will quietly keep your heart facing God.

As long as you remain in this world, you will pass from one shore to another. One season teaches you repentance. Another teaches you patience. Another opens your eyes to the wisdom of God. Do not cling too tightly to any one place along the way. Every grace prepares you for a greater grace, until at last you arrive in the city where truth is no longer sought but fully known.

Do not be distracted by every passing voice. A cloud can hide the sun for a moment, but it cannot extinguish its light. So too, many words and constant noise can veil the quiet work of God within you. Guard the silence of your heart. Read the Scriptures with prayer. Ask God Himself to reveal their living power. The deepest truths are not grasped by cleverness but received in stillness.

Remember that every act of love, every hidden sacrifice, every quiet renunciation draws the heart nearer to God. Before the soul learns to behold heavenly things, it first learns to loosen its grip on earthly ones. What begins as discipline becomes delight. What first feels like labor is slowly transformed into freedom.

Blessed is the one whose course is not turned aside by the storms of this passing world. The Lord Himself watches over your voyage. Keep your eyes upon Him, let prayer be your compass, and trust that the One who called you onto the sea will also bring you safely into the harbor of His everlasting peace.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** *Guide my journey*

> **Exhale:** *Bring me home*
