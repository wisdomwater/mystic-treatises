## 36 — The Small Walls of Watchfulness

> *Do not make peace with what quietly leads your heart away from God.*

A soul is not freed from sin merely because it dislikes the wound sin leaves behind. It must learn to hate the cause that first opens the wound. We often reject the fruit while still delighting in the root. We say we do not want the fall, yet we keep near the path that leads to it. Therefore, my beloved, be watchful not only over obvious sins, but over the small permissions that prepare a place for them.

Do not despise the little disciplines that guard the heart. Prayer, psalmody, silence, holy reading, the rhythm of the day, and the humble duties of your place may seem small, but they are walls around the soul. God will not judge you because you missed a certain number of words, as though He were counting syllables against you. But when these walls are neglected, the enemy finds an opening, and what began as small carelessness can become bitter slavery.

Freedom before discipline is dangerous. The soul that refuses to be guided by God becomes servant to whatever next captures its desire. True freedom is not doing whatever rises within you. True freedom is learning, by grace, to love what leads to life. If you surrender yourself again and again to goodness, goodness becomes strong in you. If you surrender yourself to what darkens the heart, darkness becomes a chain.

So do not deceive yourself. If you still carry the cause of sin as though it were sweet, you will not yet recognize its stench. Only when you step out of darkness do you see what darkness was doing to you. Only when you awaken from drunkenness do you understand the shame of being drunk. Confess what is wounded. Turn away from what feeds the wound. Let humility and grief become the doorway to healing.

Do not be troubled by the struggles that remain in your body or by the changing weather of your soul. Endure with patience. Moderate labor, steady prayer, and humble watchfulness are precious before God. The disturbance of this present life is brief, but the mercy prepared for you is without measure. Bear the struggle with hope, for the body that now trembles will one day be clothed in peace.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** *Guard my heart, O Lord.*

> **Exhale:** *Lead me into freedom.*
