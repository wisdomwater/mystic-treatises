## 57 — The Quiet Work of the Heart

> *A little faithfulness, practiced each day, prepares the soul for the life of God.*

Do not despise small acts of discipline because they seem insignificant. A single drop of water, falling without ceasing, wears away the hardest stone. So it is with the soul. What is done faithfully each day, though unnoticed by others, slowly opens the heart to God. Lasting transformation is not born from sudden effort but from quiet perseverance.

As the heart turns toward God, another life begins to awaken within you. The noise that once ruled your thoughts loses its power. Desires that once demanded your attention begin to fade. In their place comes a joy that cannot be explained, a sweetness that gathers the scattered mind and teaches it to rest in a deeper peace.

Guard your tongue, for silence protects the heart. Not every word needs to be spoken. When the tongue grows quiet, the heart begins to hear. You will become aware of movements within yourself that were hidden beneath constant speech. There you will discover both your weakness and God's mercy, and this knowledge will lead you, not into discouragement, but into humility and gladness.

Do not imagine that you will draw others to God chiefly by many words. A life shaped by prayer speaks more deeply than instruction alone. The person who quietly keeps watch over his own heart becomes a light for others without seeking to be seen. Your peace will encourage the troubled. Your patience will strengthen the weary. Your love will become its own testimony.

Do not fear the slow pace of this work. God delights in hidden growth. Offer Him today's small obedience, today's quiet prayer, today's guarded word, and today's faithful love. He who forms rivers from falling drops will, in His time, fill your heart with the stillness and freedom that belong to His kingdom.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** *Quiet my heart*

> **Exhale:** *Form Christ within me*
