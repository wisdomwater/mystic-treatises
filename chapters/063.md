## 63 — The Ladder and the Home

> *Knowledge may lead you toward God, but only trust allows you to rest in Him.*

Do not despise understanding, for it is one of God's gifts. It teaches you to discern, to weigh your steps, and to walk wisely through the world. Yet do not mistake the ladder for the home. Knowledge can bring you to the threshold, but it cannot give you the peace that waits beyond the door. There comes a moment when the soul must stop calculating and simply entrust itself to the faithful love of God.

Faith is not the enemy of wisdom. It is wisdom brought to its fulfillment. Knowledge asks whether the path is safe before taking a step. Faith sees the hand of God and dares to walk where no path yet exists. Knowledge is bound by what it can measure. Faith lives from the One who cannot be measured. Knowledge says, "This is possible." Faith says, "With God, all things are possible."

The heart that has tasted God's care no longer believes it must carry the weight of its own life. It does not become careless, but it becomes free. It learns to release anxious striving and entrust tomorrow to the Father who already knows every need. Such trust is not ignorance. It is the quiet confidence that grows after discovering, again and again, that God is more faithful than your own understanding.

Do not imagine that the virtues of prayer, self-denial, mercy, forgiveness, or meditation are the destination. They are precious steps, and you should practice them with diligence, for they strengthen the soul and prepare it for greater things. Yet their purpose is to lead you into a deeper communion where effort gives way to rest, where love overcomes fear, and where the heart delights in God more than in its own progress. There faith becomes your dwelling place. What once required striving becomes a gift received in silence, and the soul discovers that the One it sought has been carrying it all along.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** I trust You, Lord.

> **Exhale:** Lead me into Your rest.
