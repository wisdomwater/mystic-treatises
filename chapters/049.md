## 49 — The Hand Beneath You

> *When grace seems to withdraw, it may only be teaching you how deeply you are held.*

Do not be troubled when the struggle changes. Sometimes the enemy presses hard at the beginning, hoping to frighten the soul before it has learned the tenderness of God. At other times he waits until first zeal has grown quiet, until prayer feels ordinary, until the heart forgets the fire with which it first began.

But know this: when you turn toward God with simplicity, a hidden strength surrounds you. You may not see it. You may not feel it. Yet it stands near, guarding the soul that has entrusted itself to mercy.

Grace trains you gently. It is like a mother teaching her little child to walk. She lets the child stand a little distance away and calls him forward. His feet tremble. His courage weakens. He begins to fall. Then she runs and gathers him into her arms. So God permits you to feel your weakness, not to abandon you, but to teach you where your help truly lies.

Remember, then, the love with which you began. Remember the first warmth, the first desire, the first willingness to leave behind what could not give life. Examine your heart, not with fear, but with honesty. Has the flame grown dim? Have you laid aside the weapons of prayer, humility, Scripture, tears, and watchfulness?

Return again. Cry out to God without shame. Weep before His mercy. Ask for help until help becomes visible to the heart. Nothing is permitted without God’s care. Even the hour of testing can become a place of healing. And when you see the Savior standing with you, you will know that the struggle was never yours alone.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** *You hold me near*

> **Exhale:** *Teach me to trust*
