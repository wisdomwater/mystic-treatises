## 9 — Blessing in the Mouth

> *The humble soul conquers more by peace than by many words.*

Be willing to be thought simple by fools, but do not be despised by the wise for boldness without humility. Seek poverty because it teaches the heart to bow low. Do not seek abundance merely to make yourself strong in the eyes of others. Let your virtues answer those who criticize you, and let the peace of your lips silence what argument cannot heal.

Wherever you go, consider yourself a stranger. This will guard you from the damage that comes through careless speech. Think of yourself as one who still needs teaching, and you will remain wise throughout your life. Do not speak as a master of what you have not yet practiced. Speak, if you must, as a fellow learner, placing yourself beneath the one who listens.

Let your mouth become a servant of blessing. Scorn gives birth to scorn, but blessing gives birth to blessing. If you bless continually, another person’s contempt will not easily wound you. A peaceful tongue gathers grace around the soul, while harshness scatters what silence has gained.

When God begins to open your eyes to the mysteries hidden in creation, arm your soul with fasting, tears, humility, and restraint. Do not let knowledge make you restless or proud. Do not be drawn into arguments that feed division and slander. Read Scripture and the holy writings with quietness, not to become clever, but to be wrapped in wonder before the wisdom of the Creator.

Do not try to seize what is too high for you. Look toward the sun only as much as your eyes can bear. Taste the honey of divine things in measure, lest your soul grow weary and mistake imagination for truth. Purify your heart, clothe your thoughts with chastity and humility, and you will find God not far away, but within. To the humble, His mysteries are revealed in mercy.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** *Make my mouth a blessing.*

> **Exhale:** *Reveal Yourself in humility.*
