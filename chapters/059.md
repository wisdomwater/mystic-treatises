## 59 — Mercy Is Stronger Than Zeal

> *The heart that has learned mercy has begun to resemble God.*

Do not mistake harshness for holiness. A heart consumed by zeal has not yet found peace, and without peace the soul cannot rest in God. If you are eager to correct everyone around you while your own heart remains troubled, first turn inward. Heal yourself before you seek to heal another. The wounded need compassion more than condemnation, and so do you.

Learn the gentleness of God. When He corrects, He does not seek revenge. He disciplines only to restore what He loves. His purpose is never to repay evil with evil, but to heal His image within us. If you would become like your Father, let your corrections arise from love rather than anger, from tears rather than triumph.

Do not keep account of what others deserve. Justice measures. Mercy overflows. Justice gives according to the deed. Mercy gives according to love. Look upon the boundless compassion of God. Your sins are no greater than a handful of sand cast into the sea of His goodness. The mercy of the Creator cannot be exhausted by the failures of His creatures.

If you pray while holding resentment, your heart is divided. Forgive, and your prayer will rise unhindered. Defend the one who has fallen. Correct your brother in secret, not to shame him, but to restore him. A quiet word spoken in love heals more deeply than many true words spoken without compassion.

Seek, then, the humility that is born from love. Such humility no longer strives to win, to judge, or to be admired. It simply delights in God and becomes a place where His mercy may dwell. The more your heart is filled with compassion, the more clearly you will discover the heart of Christ within your own.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** *Fill me with Your mercy*

> **Exhale:** *Teach me to love as You love*
