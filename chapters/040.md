## 40 — Undivided in the Way

> *The cross becomes lighter when the heart has stopped looking back.*

Do not approach the way of God with a divided heart. If one part of you looks toward Christ while another keeps longing for the comforts you have left behind, your strength will soon grow weak. The Lord knows this trembling in us. He does not despise it, but He calls us beyond it: deny yourself, take up the cross, and follow.

To deny yourself is to prepare your soul as though it has already passed beyond this present life. The one who carries the cross no longer bargains with comfort as a master. He has placed his hope elsewhere. When the heart has accepted loss for the sake of love, many things that once seemed unbearable become light.

If you desire to leave old customs, do not keep standing among the things that awaken them. The body is not easily persuaded to live simply while surrounded by luxury. The heart is not easily quieted while feeding its eyes on what disturbs it. Remove the causes of your weakness when you can. This is not cowardice. It is wisdom.

Do not trust yourself too quickly. Hidden roots remain under the soil even when the summer heat makes the ground look barren. Let only a little rain fall, and they rise again. So it is with the affections. In solitude and discipline they may seem dead, but when comfort returns, they show their heads. Therefore keep watch without fear, and do not be ashamed to choose distance from what harms you.

Begin the struggle simply. Fast from what inflames you. Keep prayer with steadiness. Let the body learn that it is not the ruler of the soul. These small hardships are not hatred of life, but training for love. Christ does not call you into loss in order to leave you empty. He calls you away from what enslaves, so that your whole heart may become free for the life that never ends.

### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** *Make my heart undivided.*

> **Exhale:** *Lead me in Your way.*
