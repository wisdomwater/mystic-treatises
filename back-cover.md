There are voices that echo across the centuries because they speak to something timeless within the human soul.

Isaac of Nineveh is one of those voices.

A seventh-century monk, bishop, and contemplative, Isaac wrote with remarkable tenderness about prayer, mercy, humility, stillness, and the limitless compassion of God. His words have nourished Christians for more than a thousand years, yet the beauty of his wisdom is often hidden beneath the distance of ancient language.

This devotional brings Isaac's *Mystic Treatises* into clear, contemporary English through one hundred daily readings. Rather than offering commentary or analysis, each reading allows Isaac to speak directly to the modern reader in a voice that is gentle, spacious, and deeply pastoral.

Within these pages you will discover invitations to:

• Cultivate a deeper life of prayer and silence
• Embrace humility without shame or self-contempt
• Grow in mercy toward yourself and others
• Find hope in suffering and strength in weakness
• Learn the quiet freedom of surrender to God
• Rest more deeply in the love of Christ

These devotions are not meant to be rushed. They are invitations to slow down—to breathe, to listen, and to allow the heart to become attentive to the presence of God.

If you have been nourished by writers such as Julian of Norwich, *The Cloud of Unknowing*, Brother Lawrence, Thomas Merton, Henri Nouwen, or other voices from the Christian contemplative tradition, you will find in Isaac of Nineveh a trusted companion whose wisdom is as needed today as it was fourteen centuries ago.

Come, sit for a while with one of Christianity's great spiritual guides.

You may discover that what your soul has been seeking has been quietly waiting for you all along.
